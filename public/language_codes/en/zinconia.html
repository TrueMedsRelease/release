Common use

Zinconia (Zinc Acetate) is a nutritional supplement primarily used to treat and prevent zinc deficiency, support immune function, and aid in healing conditions like skin lesions or infections. It is also used to treat Wilsonâ€™s disease by blocking copper absorption.

Dosage and direction

Zinconia tablets should be taken orally, preferably with or immediately after meals to minimize potential gastrointestinal discomfort. The tablet should be swallowed whole with water; do not crush or chew. It is essential to strictly follow the specific dosage, frequency, and duration prescribed by a doctor.

Precautions

It is advisable to consult a doctor before using Zinconia during pregnancy and breastfeeding.
Patients with kidney diseases should use Zinc Acetate with caution, as zinc may accumulate in the body and cause toxicity. It is advisable to have regular kidney function tests while on this medication.

Contraindications

Zinconia (Zinc Acetate) is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to zinc or any of the inactive ingredients in the formulation.

Possible side effect

Side effects of Zinconia: Gastrointestinal upset, Nausea, Vomiting, Abdominal discomfort, Allergic reactions such as rash and swelling.

Drug interaction

Zinc Acetate may interact with tetracycline antibiotics (such as doxycycline and minocycline), bisphosphonates (such as alendronate) and quinolone antibiotics (such as ciprofloxacin and levofloxacin).

Missed dose

If a dose of Zinconia is missed, it should be taken as soon as it is remembered. However, if it is nearly time for the next dose, the missed dose should be skipped.

Overdose

An overdose of Zinconia may lead to symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, lethargy, and decreased urine output. Immediate medical attention should be sought if an overdose is suspected.

Storage

Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat. Keep out of the reach of children and pets.
