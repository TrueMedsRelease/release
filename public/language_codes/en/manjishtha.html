Common use

Manjishtha tablets come with detoxifying and antioxidant properties to reduce cell damage caused by free radicals. Manjishtha aids in the reduction of skin hyperpigmentation. These tablets for skin aid in detoxification by removing toxins released into the bloodstream by the liver. The major ingredient for these skincare tablets is Manjistha that aids in the reduction of skin hyperpigmentation. The plant suppresses the enzyme tyrosinase, which is responsible for the synthesis of melanin.

Dosage and direction

Take 1-2 pills twice a day, or as prescribed by your doctor.

Precautions

Before taking Manjishtha tablets, pregnant or breastfeeding women, diabetics, and those with known medical issues should visit a doctor.

Contraindications

If you are allergic to Manjishtha, Samanga or any other ingredients in Manjishtha tablets, it is advised not to take this medicine without consulting your doctor first.

Possible side effect

Because no scientific research on the topic has been done, there are no known side effects of the Manjishtha tablets. If you face adverse effects, see a doctor.

Drug interaction

Other drugs may affect Manjishtha, including prescription and over-the-counter medicines, vitamins, and herbal products. Tell your doctor about all your current medicines and any medicine you start or stop using.

Missed dose

Use the medicine as soon as you can, but skip the missed dose if it is almost time for your next dose. Do not use two doses at one time.

Overdose

Seek emergency medical attention or call you doctor.

Storage

Store at room temperature, away from moisture and heat. Keep away from children.
