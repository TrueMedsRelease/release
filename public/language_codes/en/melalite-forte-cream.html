Common use

Melalite Forte Cream is a topical medication primarily used to manage hyperpigmentation, a condition that results in dark patches on the skin. It is part of a group of medicines called skin-lightening agents. In addition to managing hyperpigmentation, it is also used for melasma, freckles, and age spots. These are all conditions that cause uneven skin tone and discoloration, making the skin lose its natural complexion.

Dosage and direction

Here's how you should use Melalite Forte Cream: Ensure the affected skin area is clean and dry before application. Apply a thin layer of the cream to the area using your fingertips. Carefully avoid any contact with your eyes, nose, or mouth. After application, wash your hands to prevent unwanted spread of the product. Do not cover the area with bandages or other coverings unless instructed by your doctor. For specific advice on frequency and duration of use, consult with your doctor.

Precautions

Caution is advised in patients with pre-existing skin conditions like eczema, and psoriasis, or those with excessive exposure to sunlight and UV therapy.
Vitiligo: In patients suffering from this condition, where skin loses its color in blotches, the use of Melalite Forte Cream could lead to unwanted skin discoloration.
Dermatitis: Individuals with dermatitis may experience increased skin irritation after using Melalite Forte Cream.

Contraindications

This medicine is not recommended during pregnancy and breastfeeding unless recommended by your doctor. Consult your doctor before using this medicine during pregnancy and breastfeeding.
Melalite Forte Cream is contraindicated in individuals who have known hypersensitivity to Hydroquinone or any other ingredients in the formulation. It should not be used on open wounds, irritated, or abraded skin.

Possible side effect

Melalite Forte Cream may have the following side effects: Mild burning sensation; Itching; Erythema (redness of the skin); Hyperpigmentation (darkening of the skin color); Skin peeling; Dryness of the skin.

Drug interaction

Benzoyl Peroxide: Using this acne treatment alongside Melalite Forte Cream can lead to temporary staining of the skin.
Resorcinol: This medicine, often present in acne and psoriasis treatments, can cause increased skin irritation when used with Melalite Forte Cream.
Sulphur: Found in certain anti-acne products, sulphur can increase skin dryness or irritation when mixed with Melalite Forte Cream.

Missed dose

If you forget to apply Melalite Forte Cream at the scheduled time, do so as soon as you remember. However, if it's nearly time for your next application, skip the missed one and continue with your regular schedule. Do not apply extra cream without talking to your doctor first.

Overdose

If you accidentally apply too much Melalite Forte Cream, do not panic. Simply wash off the excess cream with lukewarm water. If you notice any worsening symptoms or discomfort, inform your doctor.
