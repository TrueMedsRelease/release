Common use

Asthalin (Salbutamol) is primarily used to manage the symptoms of asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). It belongs to a class of medicines known as bronchodilators. In addition to these primary uses, this tablet is also used to prevent breathing problems that happen during exercise and may help with conditions like chronic bronchitis and emphysema.

Dosage and direction

Always take Asthalin as instructed by your doctor. Swallow the tablet whole with a glass of water. Avoid crushing or chewing it. Do not take more than the recommended dose without consulting your doctor.

Precautions

Consult your doctor before using this medicine in pregnancy and during breastfeeding.
If you have certain health conditions, caution and medical advice are recommended before using this medication. These include gastric or peptic ulcers, sepsis, hypokalaemia (low potassium levels in the blood), and lactic acidosis (excess acid in the bloodstream). Individuals with liver or kidney diseases, particularly cirrhosis or hepatitis, should also seek medical guidance. Other conditions requiring caution include fluid accumulation in the lungs, heart problems, an overactive thyroid, and a history of seizures (fits). Consulting a doctor before use can help prevent complications and ensure safe treatment.

Contraindications

Asthalin is not recommended in patients with a known allergy to Salbutamol and severe liver and kidney conditions.
Diabetes: If you have diabetes, monitor your blood sugar levels closely while taking this medicine as it can cause increases in blood glucose levels.
Hyperthyroidism: This tablet may increase heart rate and cause irregular heartbeat in patients with hyperthyroidism.
Cardiovascular problems: People with heart disease or high blood pressure should use the medication cautiously as it can increase heart rate and blood pressure.

Possible side effect

Common side effects of Asthalin include: Tremors; Muscle cramps; Uneven heart rate; Dizziness.

Drug interaction

Antihypertensive medications: Medications like atenolol and bisoprolol can decrease the effectiveness of Asthalin, making it less efficient in managing respiratory symptoms.
Diuretics: Using diuretics like bumetanide and furosemide with Asthalin tablet may lead to decreased potassium levels in the blood, which can cause heart rhythm problems.
Antidepressants: Certain types of antidepressants, for example, fluoxetine (Prozac) and paroxetine (Paxil, Pexeva) may increase the risk of side effects like fast or irregular heartbeat when taken with Asthalin.
Bronchodilators: When medicines like theophylline are taken with Asthalin, they may lower potassium levels in the blood.

Missed dose

If you've missed taking a dose of Asthalin, take it as soon as you remember. But if it's close to the time for your next dose, simply skip the missed one and continue with your regular schedule. Never double up on doses without consulting with your doctor.

Overdose

Accidentally taking too much of Asthalin might lead to overdose symptoms such as tremors, headaches, or an increase in heart rate. If you experience any such symptoms or suspect an overdose, contact your doctor.
