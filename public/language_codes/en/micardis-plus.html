Common use

Micardis Plus is used to manage high blood pressure, a condition also known as hypertension. This medicine is a combination product, and it falls under the category of antihypertensives. This tablet also potentially reduces the risk of severe cardiovascular events like heart attacks and strokes by effectively lowering your blood pressure.

Dosage and direction

Swallow each tablet whole with a glass of water. Avoid crushing, chewing or dividing the tablet. The medicine can be taken with or without food. However, it is important to maintain consistency with respect to food intake. Don't exceed the recommended dosage without consulting your doctor. Never stop taking this medicine without speaking to your doctor first.

Precautions

Micardis Plus is not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding due to potential risks to the fetus or infant. Consult your doctor for safer alternatives if you are pregnant or planning to conceive.
Caution is advised in patients with kidney disease, liver disease, diabetes, electrolyte disturbances, or those with a history of gout. Regular monitoring of blood pressure, kidney function, and electrolyte levels is recommended.

Contraindications

Micardis Plus is contraindicated in patients with a history of allergy to either ingredient (Hydrochlorothiazide or Telmisartan), anuria (inability to produce urine), severe liver impairment, or in women who are pregnant or breastfeeding.

Possible side effect

Side effects of Micardis Plus: Dizziness; Fatigue; Hypotension (low blood pressure); Increased urination; Electrolyte imbalance (low potassium or sodium levels).

Drug interaction

Avoid using potassium supplements, potassium-sparing diuretics (spironolactone), lithium, NSAIDs (ibuprofen, diclofenac), other antihypertensives (enalapril, lisinopril), and certain antidiabetics (insulin, glipizide). These can interact with Micardis Plus, potentially affecting its safety and efficacy.

Missed dose

If you forget to take a dose of Micardis Plus tablet, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it's close to your next dose, simply skip the missed dose and continue with your regular schedule. Please refrain from increasing your dosage without first consulting your doctor.

Overdose

Overdosing on Micardis Plus could result in common side effects becoming more pronounced, such as dizziness or diarrhea. If you suspect an overdose, it is crucial to inform your doctor to discuss the next steps.
