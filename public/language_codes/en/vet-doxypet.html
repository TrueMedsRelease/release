Common use

Doxypet tablets is a medicine used in pets for treating bacterial infections, including those affecting the respiratory tract, such as rhinitis, tonsillitis, and bronchopneumonia. It kills and stops the bacteria from multiplying. This helps clear the infections and speeds up recovery.

Dosage and direction

Doxypet should be given exactly as prescribed by the veterinarian. The dose and duration of the treatment may depend on body weight, type, or severity of the infection. Always follow your veterinarianâ€™s dosage instructions.

Precautions

Use with caution in young animals, as this medicine may cause tooth discoloration if given during tooth development.

Contraindications

Doxypet tablets is not recommended for use in pregnant or lactating animals and should not be used in pets known to be hypersensitive to doxycycline or other tetracyclines.

Possible side effect

There is limited data on the common side effects of Doxypet. Some dogs may develop skin sensitivity to sunlight (photodermatitis) after exposure to strong sunlight during treatment. Vomiting and swelling, irritation, or inflammation of the esophagus may also occur in some animals.

Drug interaction

Doxypet should not be used with other antibiotics. Additionally, avoid giving the medicine along with iron supplements, antacids, or oral absorbents, as they can interfere with absorption. Exposure to intense sunlight or ultraviolet light should be minimized during treatment, as this medicine may cause skin sensitivity (photodermatitis).

Missed dose

Give the medicine as soon as you can, but skip the missed dose if it is almost time for your next dose. Do not give two doses at one time.

Overdose

Seek your veterinarian for emergency medical attention.

Storage

Store at room temperature away from moisture and heat. Keep out of reach of the children and pets.
