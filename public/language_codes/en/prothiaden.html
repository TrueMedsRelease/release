Common use

Prothiaden is a medicine used to manage both depression and anxiety. It is primarily used to manage depression, a condition characterized by persistent and prolonged feelings of sadness and loss of interest. It is also effective in reducing symptoms of anxiety, which is excessive worry and fear about everyday situations. This medicine is typically recommended when other treatments have not been successful.

Dosage and direction

Take Prothiaden tablets as directed by your doctor. Swallow the tablet whole with a glass of water. Do not crush or chew the tablet. Consult your doctor for specific advice on dosage and duration of treatment.

Precautions

If you have any planned surgeries, intolerance to certain sugars, or thyroid problems, inform your doctor before starting Prothiaden.
If you are pregnant or planning to become pregnant, it is important to consult your doctor before taking Prothiaden tablets. They will assess the benefits and risks and determine if it is suitable for you.
If you are breastfeeding, it is advisable to consult your doctor before using Prothiaden. They will consider the potential risks and benefits to both you and your baby.
Inform your doctor about any known allergies or hypersensitivity reactions to dosulepin or other similar medications.

Contraindications

Liver disease: Prothiaden tablet is not suitable for individuals with liver problems, as it can worsen the condition.
Heart conditions: If you have an irregular heartbeat or other heart conditions, consult your doctor before taking Prothiaden.
Glaucoma: Individuals with glaucoma should avoid using Prothiaden tablet, as it can increase eye pressure.
Prothiaden tablet is not recommended for children and adolescents under the age of 18.

Possible side effect

Side Effects of Prothiaden: Dry mouth: You may experience a dry sensation in your mouth; Blurred vision: Your vision may become unclear or blurry; Dizziness: You might feel lightheaded or unsteady on your feet; Constipation: You may have difficulty passing stools; Sedation: You may feel more sleepy or drowsy than usual.

Drug interaction

Antidepressants: Avoid combining Prothiaden with other antidepressant medications such as fluoxetine or sertraline, as it can increase the risk of side effects.
Monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs): Do not take Prothiaden if you are currently taking or have recently taken an MAOI medication, as it can cause a serious reaction.
Anticoagulants: Prothiaden may interact with anticoagulant medications like warfarin, increasing the risk of bleeding.
Antiarrhythmics: Certain antiarrhythmic medications like amiodarone or quinidine can interact with Prothiaden and affect heart rhythm.
Benzodiazepines: Combining Prothiaden with benzodiazepines like diazepam or lorazepam may increase sedation.

Missed dose

If you forget to take Prothiaden tablet as instructed, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it's almost time for your next dose, skip the missed one and continue with your regular dosage schedule.

Overdose

Taking too much Prothiaden can be dangerous and may lead to serious side effects. If you or someone else accidentally takes an overdose, seek immediate medical attention.

Storage

Store Prothiaden at room temperature. Keep the medicine in a cool and dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Make sure to store it in its original packaging or container. Keep Prothiaden out of reach and sight of children. Do not use the medicine after the expiry date mentioned on the packaging.
