Common use

Diane 35 tablet is primarily used for managing polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS). This is a combination medicine that falls under the category of hormonal contraceptives. In addition to its primary function, this medicine also serves as an effective hormonal contraceptive. Its unique blend of ingredients helps in regulating menstrual cycles and reducing symptoms associated with PCOS, such as irregular periods, unwanted facial or body hair, and acne.

Dosage and direction

Thoroughly follow the dosage and timing guidelines provided by your doctor for taking this medicine. Do not change or exceed the prescribed dose without consulting your doctor. Avoid discontinuing this medication without your doctor's advice. Always consult your doctor before stopping. Adhere to the diet plan recommended by your doctor.

Precautions

Diane 35 is not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding. If you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding, consult your doctor before using this medication.
Women with a history of liver disease or cancer should use Diane 35 with caution. Also, women with a history of blood clots or stroke should consult their doctor before using this medication.
Obesity: Individuals who are obese should be cautious as this medication may exacerbate their condition.

Contraindications

Diane 35 is contraindicated in women who are pregnant or breastfeeding, smoke and are over 35 years old, have a history of blood clots, stroke, or heart attack, have severe liver disease, have certain types of migraine, have or have had breast cancer or other estrogen-sensitive cancers, and have undiagnosed vaginal bleeding.
Cardiovascular Diseases: Diane 35 tablet might pose a risk to individuals suffering from heart-related diseases.

Possible side effect

Diane 35 tablet may have the following common side effects: Nausea; Stomach pain; Headache; Weight gain; Breast pain; Mood changes.

Drug interaction

Anticoagulants: Medicines such as warfarin or heparin may interact with Diane 35 tablet, potentially altering the action of these blood thinners.
Anti-HIV Medications: Medicines like ritonavir or efavirenz can affect the hormone levels in your body when taken with Diane 35.
Antiepileptics: Medicines like phenytoin and carbamazepine can reduce the effectiveness of Diane 35 tablet.

Missed dose

If you forget to take your regular dose of Diane 35 tablet, take it as soon as you remember. If it's nearly time for your next dose, skip the missed one and continue with your usual dosing schedule. Never increase your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

An overdose of Diane 35 tablet can lead to enhanced side effects such as nausea, stomach pain, and headaches. If you have taken more than the prescribed dose, inform your doctor about the situation.
