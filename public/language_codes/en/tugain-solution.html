Common use

Tugain Solution is primarily used to manage hair loss, specifically a condition known as androgenetic alopecia. This topical medicine belongs to a class of drugs known as vasodilators. In addition to addressing hair loss, this solution is also beneficial in promoting hair regrowth. It is particularly efficient in individuals experiencing pattern baldness, an issue characterized by gradual thinning or bald patches on the top of the scalp.

Dosage and direction

Ensure your scalp and hair are dry before application. Apply the solution directly to your scalp, focusing on the areas where hair thinning or loss is most severe. Use your fingertips to gently spread the solution evenly across your scalp. After applying, let your hair dry naturally. Avoid using a hairdryer as it may reduce the effectiveness of the medicine. Always wash your hands thoroughly after using this solution to avoid accidental transfer to other parts of your body.

Precautions

Cardiovascular disease: Patients with heart-related ailments should use Tugain Solution cautiously as it may exacerbate symptoms.
Skin conditions: Those suffering from skin disorders should exercise caution while using Tugain Solution, as it could potentially worsen their condition.

Contraindications

Tugain Solution is contraindicated in individuals who have a known hypersensitivity to either of its two components: minoxidil or ethanol.
Tugain Solution is not recommended for children under 18 years of age. Always consult your doctor before giving any medicine to a child.

Possible side effect

Side effects of Tugain Solution: Irritation of the scalp; Dryness of the scalp; Scaling or flaking of the scalp; Itching of the scalp; Burning sensation on the scalp.

Drug interaction

Tugain Solution may interact unfavourably when combined with other topical treatments for the scalp like antifungal creams (like ketoconazole), topical corticosteroids (e.g., betamethasone) may lead to skin irritation or altered effectiveness of both products.

Missed dose

In case of a missed dose, continue with the next scheduled dose. Do not attempt to make up for the missed dose by applying the extra solution.

Overdose

An overdose of Tugain Solution may result in severe side effects such as dizziness or rapid heartbeat. If you suspect an overdose, seek immediate medical attention.

Storage

Store Tugain Solution in a cool place. Keep it away from direct sunlight and moisture to maintain its effectiveness. Ensure it is out of reach of children to prevent accidental consumption. Always check the expiry date before using the solution.
