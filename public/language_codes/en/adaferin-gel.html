Common use

Adaferin Gel belongs to a class of medications called retinoid-like compounds. It is primarily used to control acne in adults and children who are more than 12 years of age. It prevents the development of tiny pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads. It reduces the excessive oil production, irritation, and unclogs the clogged pores.

Dosage and direction

It is important to follow the recommended directions for use. Here's a step-by-step guide on using Adaferin gel:
Cleanse the affected area with a gentle cleanser and pat dry.
Wash your hands thoroughly before and after application.
Apply a small amount of Adaferin gel evenly to the affected area.
Avoid contact with the eyes, lips, and mucous membranes.
Use Adaferin gel regularly as prescribed by your doctor.
Avoid exposure to sunlight, sunlamps and if it cannot be avoided, wear sunscreen.

Precautions

Before applying the gel, inform the doctor about your pre-existing medical conditions and current medications. Adhere to the prescribed dosage and schedule, and promptly report any encountered side effects.

Contraindications

Eczema (a skin disease) - Adaferin gel may worsen the skin condition.
Sunburned skin - The gel may worsen the irritation and lead to increased discomfort, redness, and peeling.
If you are allergic to Adaferin gel or its ingredients, do not use this gel.
Adaferin gel should not be used in pregnancy. So, inform your doctor if you are pregnant or planning to become pregnant.

Possible side effect

Common side effects of Adaferin gel may include: Redness; Dryness; Scaly skin; Mild burning or stinging; Pain at the application site.

Drug interaction

Adaferin gel should not be used simultaneously with other Retinoids.

Missed dose

If you forget to apply Adaferin gel at the scheduled time, apply it as soon as you remember. If it is close to the time for your next dose, skip the missed dose and continue with your regular dosing schedule. Do not apply extra gel to make up for the missed dose unless instructed by your doctor.

Overdose

Excessive application of Adaferin gel can lead to marked redness, scaling and skin discomfort. Inform your doctor regarding these effects and refrain from further application until directed to do so by your doctor.
