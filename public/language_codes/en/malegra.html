Common use

Malegra is prescribed to men to treat erectile dysfunction (impotence). Erectile dysfunction is the inability to obtain and maintain a firm enough erection for sexual activity. Sildenafil Citrate is the main active ingredient of Malegra, which belongs to a family of drugs called phosphodiesterase-5 (PDE-5) inhibitor. This medicine works by helps to relax blood vessels and increases blood flow to particular areas of the body.

Dosage and direction

Malegra is a prescription drug and should always be used as prescribed by the doctor. Here are some general guidelines to follow on how to take: Take this ED pill 30-60 minutes before engaging in sexual activity; Swallow a whole tablet without crushing, breaking, or chewing it; You can take Malegra tablets on an empty stomach or after a light meal; Do not take more than one Malegra dosage in 24 hours.

Precautions

It is important to note that if you have any of the following diseases, you should consult your doctor before taking this medicine: Cardiovascular disease: Heart disease, Angina; Retinal disorders: Retinitis pigmentosa; Kidney disease: Renal impairment.

Contraindications

If you have severe heart or liver problems, recently had a stroke or heart attack, or have low blood pressure, it is important to avoid using Malegra. It is contraindicated in patients with a history of hypersensitivity to Sildenafil.

Possible side effect

Taking Malegra tablets may cause some side effects. Here are a few common ones to be aware of: Flushing (sense of warmth); Headache; Dizziness; Blurred vision; Muscle pain.

Drug interaction

Certain medications can interact with Malegra. Here are some categories of medications and specific examples: Nitrates: Nitroglycerin, Isosorbide dinitrate; Alpha-blockers: Tamsulosin, Doxazosin; Antifungals: Ketoconazole, Itraconazole; Antibiotics: Erythromycin, Clarithromycin.

Missed dose

If you forget to take Malegra tablet as instructed, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it is almost time for your next dose, skip the missed dose and continue with your regular dosing schedule. Never increase your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

If you accidentally take too much Malegra, seek immediate medical attention. Taking too much of this medicine can lead to severe side effects such as a prolonged and painful erection (priapism). Contact your doctor or visit a nearby hospital if you experience any discomfort or unusual symptoms.

Storage

Store Malegra in a dry, hygienic, and cool place, away from direct sunlight and out of the reach of children and pets.
