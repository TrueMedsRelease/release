Common use

Isotroin are a medicine used to manage severe recalcitrant nodular acne that has not responded to other treatment options like antibiotics.

Dosage and direction

Take orally with a full glass of water. The dosage and duration of treatment will vary based on individual factors such as weight, severity of acne, and response to treatment. Consult your doctor for specific advice on how to take this medicine.

Precautions

Depression: Isotretinoin can worsen symptoms of depression, so caution is advised in patients with a history of depression or mental health disorders.
Liver disease: Isotretinoin may affect liver function, so it should be used with caution in patients with liver disease or impaired liver function.
Diabetes: Isotretinoin can affect blood sugar levels and may require adjustments in diabetes medication dosages.
Hyperlipidemia (high cholesterol): Isotretinoin may increase lipid levels in the blood and should be used cautiously in patients with high cholesterol.

Contraindications

If you are pregnant or planning a baby, inform your doctor before taking Isotroin. This medication may harm the unborn baby.
If you are breastfeeding, it is advisable to consult with your doctor before taking Isotroin.
Inform your doctor if you are allergic to any of the ingredients in the Isotroin. Your doctor will prescribe an alternative medication if necessary.
Isotroin should not be used in children under the age of 12 without consulting a paediatrician.

Possible side effect

Some of the most common side effects of Isotroin: Headache: You may develop a mild headache that usually goes away on its own; Dry eyes: Your eyes may become dry and irritated, and this may cause blurred vision; Dry skin: Your skin may become dry and cracked, and this could lead to itching or a rash; Nasal dryness: Your nose might feel dry and stuffy.

Drug interaction

Isotroin may interact with certain medicines, including: Antibiotics: Tetracycline, Minocycline; Corticosteroids: Prednisone, Dexamethasone; Vitamin A supplements: Retinol, Beta-carotene; Seizure medications: Phenytoin, Carbamazepine.

Missed dose

If you forget to take Isotroin as instructed, take it as soon as you remember. If it's almost time for your next dose, skip the missed one now and resume your regular dosage schedule. Never increase your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

If you accidentally take too many Isotroin, seek immediate medical attention. Common side effects of an overdose may include vomiting, headache, and stomach pain.

Storage

Store Isotroin in a cool, dry place. Keep the medication away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep the medicine out of reach of children and pets. Use the product before its expiration date.
