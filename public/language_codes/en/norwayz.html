Common use

Norwayz is a prescription drug that is used to treat Friedreich's ataxia and vision impairment in people who have Leber's hereditary optic neuropathy, an eye illness that can result in blindness. In this way, it aids in the restoration of lost vision.

Dosage and direction

Follow your doctor's instructions on the dosage and duration of this medicine. It should not be chewed, crushed, or broken. Norwayz tablet should be consumed with food. It is critical that you do not stop taking this medicine without first consulting your doctor.

Precautions

Patients with history of seizures, kidney problems, liver problems, heart conditions, and high blood pressure should exercise caution when taking Norwayz. Regular monitoring of liver and kidney function as well heart rate and blood pressure is advised during treatment.

Contraindications

Norwayz is contraindicated in patients with known allergies to Idebenone.
This medicine is contraindicated in patients with severe liver or kidney impairment and severe heart disease.

Possible side effect

The majority of side effects are minor and will go away as your body adjusts to the medicine. If they don't go away or you're worried about them, see your doctor. Adverse effects of Norwayz tablet: Nasopharyngitis (inflammation of the throat and nasal passages); Cough; Diarrhea; Back pain.

Drug interaction

Norwayz may interact with health supplements (such as coenzyme Q10), antioxidants (like vitamin E), and anticoagulants (such as warfarin). Before starting any prescription or over-the-counter medications, it's crucial to consult a doctor.

Missed dose

If you forget to take this medicine, take it as soon as you remember. Avoid repeating the dose if it's close to your next scheduled dose.

Overdose

An overdose of Norwayz may cause symptoms such as dizziness, headache, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, and rapid heartbeat. If you observe these symptoms, inform your doctor and follow the advice accordingly.

Storage

Store Norwayz it at a room temperature between 20 C and 25 C. Avoid exposing the medication to excessive heat, humidity, or direct sunlight. Always keep this medicine out of reach of children and pets.
