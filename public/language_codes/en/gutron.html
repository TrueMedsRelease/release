Common use

Gutron is primarily used to manage orthostatic hypotension a condition where your blood pressure significantly drops when you stand up, causing dizziness and potential fainting. This medicine belongs to a class of medications known as sympathomimetic amines. In addition to managing orthostatic hypotension, this medicine is beneficial for patients with autonomic dysfunction. This includes conditions like Parkinson's disease, multiple system atrophy, and pure autonomic failure, where the body's automatic functions are impaired.

Dosage and direction

Follow the dosage and timing instructions provided by your doctor precisely. Do not change or exceed the prescribed dose without consulting your doctor. Consult your doctor before discontinuing the medication; do not stop taking it without their guidance. Adhere to the dietary recommendations given by your doctor.

Precautions

It is advised that pregnant women and breastfeeding mothers use Gutron only if the potential benefits outweigh the potential risks. Please consult your doctor before using Gutron if you are pregnant or breastfeeding.
Gutron should be used with caution in patients with severe heart disease, acute kidney injury, urine retention problems, pheochromocytoma (a rare tumour that causes high blood pressure), or thyrotoxicosis (an overactive thyroid gland). Elderly patients and those with liver or kidney problems should use Gutron with caution. In these cases, dose adjustments may be necessary.

Contraindications

Gutron should not be used in patients with certain medical conditions like high blood pressure when they are lying down, those who have an allergy to Midodrine or any of its components, and those who are already taking some types of vasoconstrictor agents or monoamine oxidase (MAO) inhibitors, substances that can interact with Midodrine and potentially cause harmful effects.

Possible side effect

Gutron may cause the following common side effects: Itching; Goosebumps; Tingling sensation; Chills; Urinary retention; Elevated blood pressure while lying down, also known as supine hypertension.

Drug interaction

Gutron (Midodrine) may interact with antihistamines (e.g., diphenhydramine), anticonvulsants (e.g., pregabalin), and antipsychotics (e.g., quetiapine).

Missed dose

If you forget to take your Gutron tablet at the required time, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it's almost time for your next dose, leave out the missed dose and continue with your regular schedule. Never increase your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

If you accidentally take more Gutron than you should, you may experience symptoms such as an increase in body hair, a tingling sensation, or chills. If these symptoms persist or worsen, inform your doctor.

Storage

Keep Gutron in its original packaging. Store at room temperature, below 25°C. Protect the medicine from light and moisture exposure. Keep the medicine out of reach of children and pets.
