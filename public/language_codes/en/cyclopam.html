Common use

Cyclopam tablets is used to relieve painful muscle spasms in the stomach and intestines. It is commonly prescribed for abdominal cramps, colicky pain, period pain (dysmenorrhea), and irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). The medicine works by relaxing smooth muscles and blocking pain signals, providing fast relief from stomach discomfort caused by intestinal spasms and abdominal pain.

Dosage and direction

Take it exactly as prescribed and avoid long-term use without medical guidance. Cyclopam should be taken orally with food or after food to reduce the risk of stomach upset. Cyclopam tablet should be swallowed whole with water. Do not crush or chew the tablet.

Precautions

Inform your doctor if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, or have conditions such as glaucoma, prostate problems, or liver disease. Patients with kidney or liver disease should inform their healthcare provider before taking this medicine.
This tablet should be used cautiously in patients with prostate enlargement, as it may exacerbate symptoms and increase the risk of urinary retention.

Contraindications

If you have an allergic reaction (hypersensitivity) to Dicyclomine hydrochloride and Paracetamol, do not take Cyclopam tablet.
Gastrointestinal conditions (such as ulcerative colitis, inflammatory bowel disease, or intestinal obstruction). Cyclopam may worsen these conditions by decreasing intestinal motility and exacerbating symptoms.

Possible side effect

Cyclopam may cause dry mouth, blurred vision, dizziness, mild nausea, sleepiness, constipation, and stomach discomfort. Seek immediate help for severe allergic reactions.

Drug interaction

Cyclopam can interact with medications, including: Antacids (take antacids at least two hours before or after Cyclopam); Benzodiazepines (can increase sedation and drowsiness); Monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs); Opioid pain medications.

Missed dose

If you forgot to take the Cyclopam, take it as soon as you remember. If the time for the next dosage is close, skipping the missed one is recommended now and resume your regular dosage pattern. Never increase your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

If you or someone else takes an excessive amount of Cyclopam by accident, it is essential to take immediate medical attention from your doctor or nearby hospital. Symptoms of an overdose include dry mouth, blurred vision, dizziness, nausea and vomiting, drowsiness, and allergic reaction.

Storage

Store the tablets at room temperature (15-25ºC). Protect it from extreme light and moisture. Keep Cyclopam away from children and pets.
