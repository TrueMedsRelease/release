Common use

Zyhcg Injection is primarily used to manage infertility in both men and women. This medicine is a part of the gonadotropin group of medications that are typically used in reproductive health treatment.
Apart from this primary use, it aids the normal growth of eggs within the ovaries and encourages ovulation in women. For men, it serves to enhance sperm production. It also plays a significant role in promoting normal sexual development in boys by assisting in the proper functioning of the testicles and sexual organs.

Dosage and direction

This medicine is administered via an intramuscular injection by a healthcare professional.
Patients should not attempt to self-inject this medication.
The injection must be given at a healthcare facility under the supervision of a medical professional.
Remember, it's important not to stop using this medicine without consulting your doctor first.

Precautions

Caution is advised while taking Zyhcg Injection if you are pregnant. It is best to consult your doctor before taking this medicine.
Caution is advised when consuming alcohol if this injection is prescribed to you to avoid any adverse effects.
Caution is advised while taking Zyhcg Injection if you have liver health issues. It is a good idea to consult your doctor before taking this medicine.
Similar caution is required if you have a history of kidney problems. Always discuss such health issues with your doctor before starting on Zyhcg.
Zyhcg Injection should not be given to children under 18 years of age without medical advice. Always consult your doctor before giving any medicine to a child.

Contraindications

Zyhcg Injection is contraindicated for breastfeeding/lactating women. Consult your doctor to understand the potential risks and benefits.
If you have an allergy to human chorionic gonadotropin or any other component of Zyhcg, you must inform your doctor before starting this medication.
Tumours of the hypothalamus or pituitary gland: The medication could exacerbate these conditions.
Ovarian cysts or enlargement not due to polycystic ovary syndrome: This medication may increase the size of ovarian cysts.

Possible side effect

Zyhcg may have the following common side effects: Pain, redness, or swelling at the injection site; Headache; Fatigue; Nausea and vomiting; Abdominal discomfort; Mood changes.
Most side effects are temporary and generally harmless, discontinuing the use of Zyhcg Injection. However, if you experience any serious side effects or worsening of any of the symptoms, please consult your doctor.

Drug interaction

Zyhcg interacts with the following medicines:
Corticosteroids: Medicines like dexamethasone and prednisone could potentially increase or decrease the effect of Zyhcg.
Thyroid hormones: Medications like levothyroxine and liothyronine may interact with Zyhcg, affecting thyroid hormone levels.

Missed dose

In case you miss a dose of Zyhcg Injection, ensure to administer it as soon as you recall. If the time for your next dose is near, skip the missed dose and continue with your regular schedule. Do not alter your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

Overdose of this injection is unlikely as it is administered by a healthcare professional.
