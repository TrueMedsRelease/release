Common use

Viropil tablets is used for the management of HIV-1 infection in both adults and children. This combination antiretroviral medication is effective in initiating treatment for HIV-1 in patients who have never been treated before or as a replacement for those with suppressed viral loads and no history of treatment failure. The active ingredients in Viropil help prevent viral replication, thus improving immune function and reducing the viral load in the blood.

Dosage and direction

Swallow the tablet whole with a glass of water. Do not crush or chew it. You can take this medicine with or without food. Do not take more than the recommended dose without consulting your doctor. If you decide to stop taking this medicine, ensure you first consult your doctor.

Precautions

Caution is advised while taking Viropil tablet if you are pregnant. It is best to consult your doctor before taking this medicine.
All patients should be tested for hepatitis B virus (HBV) before starting with Viropil as severe acute exacerbations of hepatitis B have been reported. If you have liver problems, discuss them with your doctor before starting Viropil.
Caution is required for using Viropil tablet if you have a history of any kidney problems. The medicine is not recommended in patients with severe renal impairment or end-stage renal disease requiring dialysis.

Contraindications

If you are allergic to any ingredients in Viropil, discontinue its use immediately and consult your doctor.
If you have severe kidney problems or end-stage renal disease requiring dialysis, Viropil tablet is not recommended as it may worsen your condition.
Viropil is contraindicated for breastfeeding or lactating women. Consult your doctor to understand the potential risks and benefits.

Possible side effect

Viropil may cause insomnia, headache, and fatigue. Consult your doctor if symptoms worsen.

Drug interaction

Viropil interacts with the following medicines: Anti-epileptic medicines: Medications like carbamazepine, oxcarbazepine, phenobarbital, and phenytoin; Tuberculosis (TB) medication: Rifampin, a TB medication; St. John's wort, a herbal supplement often used for depression; Antiviral medicines: Some antiviral medications like adefovir, dipivoxil and cidofovir.

Missed dose

If you forget to take a dose of Viropil, take it as soon as you remember. But if it's almost time for your next dose, skip the one you missed and continue with your regular schedule. Remember not to increase your dosage without speaking to your doctor.

Overdose

If you accidentally take more Viropil than you should inform your doctor.

Storage

Store Viropil below 30°C, in a cool, dry place. Avoid exposure to excessive light and moisture. Keep the tablet bottle tightly closed when not in use. Keep out of reach of children and pets.
