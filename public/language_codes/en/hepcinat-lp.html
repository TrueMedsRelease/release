Common use

Hepcinat LP is primarily used for managing chronic hepatitis C virus (HCV) infection. It belongs to the group of antiviral medicines. The medicine is also prescribed to manage HCV genotype 1 infection in patients with advanced liver disease, including decompensated cirrhosis.

Dosage and direction

The medication is generally taken orally once daily, either with or without food. The tablet should be swallowed whole with water; it should not be crushed, chewed, or broken. It is essential to take this medication at the same time every day for the full duration prescribed by a healthcare provider.

Precautions

Patients with decompensated cirrhosis should exercise caution when taking Hepcinat LP. It is always advised to consult your doctor before using this medicine during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

Contraindications

Hepcinat LP (Ledipasvir and Sofosbuvir) is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to ledipasvir or sofosbuvir. It is not recommended for children under 3 years old, and caution is needed for those with severe kidney disease or dialysis.

Possible side effect

Side effects of Hepcinat LP: Fatigue; Headache; Nausea; Insomnia; Diarrhea.

Drug interaction

Hepcinat LP (Ledipasvir and Sofosbuvir) may interact with antibiotics (such as rifampin) and herbal medicines (St. John's wort).

Missed dose

In case of a missed dose, take it as soon as you remember on the same day. However, do not double the dose if it is near the time for your next dose.

Overdose

If an overdose of Hepcinat LP is suspected, seek immediate medical attention.

Storage

The Hepcinat LP medicine should be kept in a cool, dry place, out of reach of children and pets.
