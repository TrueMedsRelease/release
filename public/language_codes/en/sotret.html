Common use

Sotret capsule is a medicine used to manage severe forms of acne that have not responded to other treatments. This medicine can help reduce the production of sebum, a natural substance that causes acne. It also kills the bacteria that cause acne and prevents the formation of spots or pimples. Sotret capsule has anti-inflammatory properties that help decrease inflammation, redness, and swelling caused by acne.

Dosage and direction

Take it with food: It's important to take this medicine with food to enhance absorption. Try to take it at the same time every day. Consult your doctor: Before starting treatment with Sotret capsule, consult your doctor for specific advice on dosage and duration. Regular use is crucial: It may take several weeks for the medication to show improvement in your condition. Be patient and take it regularly as prescribed.

Precautions

Caution is advised in patients with a history of liver disease, high cholesterol levels, osteoporosis, inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), and psychiatric disorders such as depression or anxiety. Regular blood tests may be required to monitor liver function, cholesterol levels, and other parameters during treatment.

Contraindications

Sotret (Isotretinoin) is strictly contraindicated in pregnancy due to its high risk of causing severe birth defects. It should not be used by individuals with liver disease, high cholesterol levels, severe depression, or allergy to isotretinoin or vitamin A derivatives.

Possible side effect

Taking Sotret capsule may cause certain side effects. Here are some common side effects you may experience: Dry skin; Chapped lips; Dry eyes; Increased sensitivity to sunlight; Nosebleeds; Muscle or joint pain; Headache; Mood changes (rare but serious); Liver enzyme abnormalities (rare but requires monitoring).

Drug interaction

Certain antibiotics, such as tetracycline and minocycline, may interact with Sotret. Also medications containing vitamin A or retinoids should be avoided while taking Sotret capsule.

Missed dose

If you forget to take a dose of Sotret capsule, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it's almost time for your next scheduled dose, skip the missed one and continue with your regular dosing schedule.

Overdose

Taking too much Sotret can lead to overdose symptoms such as headache, vomiting, stomach pain, dizziness, and dry skin. If you accidentally take too much, seek immediate medical attention.

Storage

Store Sotret capsule in a cool, dry place. Protect the medicine from light and moisture by keeping it in its original packaging. Keep Sotret capsule out of reach and sight of children.
