Common use

Nasonex Nasal Spray treats allergy symptoms such as sneezing, itching, or runny or stuffy nose. It may also be used to treat nasal polyps. It works by decreasing inflammation in the nose, making it easier to breathe. It belongs to a group of medications called nasal steroids.

Dosage and direction

To administer Nasonex Nasal Spray properly, follow these steps:
Clear your nostrils by blowing your nose gently.
Shake the bottle thoroughly before each use.
Remove the cap and hold the bottle upright.
Slightly tilt your head forward, insert the nozzle into one nostril pointing it towards the outer wall of your nose.
Close the other nostril with a finger and spray the medicine while inhaling gently through your nose.
Try not to sneeze or blow your nose immediately after using the spray.
For specific advice on how to use this medicine, consult your doctor.

Precautions

Take precautions when using Nasonex Nasal Spray if you are pregnant. It is best to consult your doctor before starting this medicine.
If you are breastfeeding, consult your doctor before using Nasonex Nasal Spray, as it is not known whether the active ingredients can pass into breast milk.
Those with liver health issues should exercise caution when using this medicine. It is advisable to discuss these concerns with your doctor before starting Nasonex Nasal Spray.
If you have kidney health issues, caution is advised when using Nasonex Nasal Spray. It's a good idea to consult your doctor before starting this medicine.
Nasonex Nasal Spray may induce fatigue, dizziness, and vision disturbances, impacting alertness. In such instances, refrain from driving or operating machinery.
Consult your doctor before using Nasonex Nasal Spray if you are below 18 years of age, as it is not recommended for children and adolescents.

Contraindications

Untreated infections in the nose, such as tuberculosis, herpes, or fungal infection: Using Nasonex Nasal Spray may worsen the infection.
Recent nose injury or operation: It is advisable to avoid using Nasonex Nasal Spray until the nose has healed properly.
Taking corticosteroids by mouth.
An unusual or allergic reaction to mometasone, other corticosteroids, medications, foods, dyes, or preservatives.
Pregnant or trying to get pregnant.
Breast-feeding.

Possible side effect

Common side effects of Nasonex Nasal Spray include: Nose irritation; Runny nose; Irritation; Headache.
Most side effects are temporary and generally harmless, which resolve on discontinuing Nasonex Nasal Spray. However, please consult your doctor if you experience severe side effects or worsening symptoms.

Drug interaction

Nasonex Nasal Spray may interact with the following medications, including:
Other corticosteroids (Hydrocortisone, Prednisone): Increases the risk of systemic corticosteroid side effects, but no significant impact on efficacy.
Antifungal medications (Ketoconazole, Itraconazole): Increases the efficacy of this medicine by reducing its breakdown, potentially leading to higher systemic steroid levels.
HIV protease inhibitors (Atazanavir, Ritonavir): Increases the efficacy of this medicine due to decreased metabolism, raising the risk of systemic side effects.

Missed dose

If you miss a dose of Nasonex Nasal Spray, take it as soon as you remember. However, avoid repeating the dose.

Overdose

If you use more Nasonex Nasal Spray than advised, you might experience some common side effects more intensely, like a distorted sense of taste or headaches. If you observe this, it would be wise to inform your doctor.

Storage

Keep the in-use bottle at room temperature, stored in an upright position, within the range of 15°C–30°C (59°F–86°F), for a duration of up to 35 days. Each bottle is designed to provide at least 30 doses. Please protect it from light and moisture. Keep away from children. Do not use this medicine after the expiry date has passed.
