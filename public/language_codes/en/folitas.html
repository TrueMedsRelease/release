Common use

Folitas (Folic Acid) is a vital nutrient used to treat anaemia caused by a deficiency of folic acid in both adults and children. It is commonly prescribed during pregnancy, in cases where red blood cells are damaged, and for patients undergoing kidney dialysis who may experience reduced folic acid levels. This essential nutritional supplement also plays a crucial role in preventing spina bifida, a serious congenital abnormality affecting the spine, in babies during pregnancy.

Dosage and direction

Typically taken daily as advised by a healthcare professional. Should be swallowed whole with water, with or without food, based on instructions from a healthcare provider. It is important not to chew or crush the tablet. For consistent blood levels, it is often advised to take it at the same time each day.

Precautions

Folitas can be taken during pregnancy. If you are breastfeeding, please speak with your doctor before taking this medicine.
Caution is advised in patients undergoing haemodialysis due to kidney failure, if you are receiving a coronary stent (a tube that is inserted in the artery that leads to the heart to stop blockages), if you suffer from pernicious anaemia (a disorder preventing the absorption of vitamin B12) or a lack of vitamin B12, and if you have a tumour.

Contraindications

This medicine is contraindicated in patients with a history of allergy to folic acid and in cancer patients.

Possible side effect

Side effects of Folitas: Loss of appetite, Nausea, Bloating of the stomach, Flatulence.

Drug interaction

Avoid taking antibiotics (trimethoprim and sulphonamides), tumour medications (Fluorouracil), antacids containing aluminium or magnesium and anticonvulsants (phenytoin), medications to treat inflammatory bowel conditions, ulcerative colitis and Crohn's disease, (sulfasalazine), cholesterol-lowering medications (cholestyramine), preparations containing zinc such as vitamins or food supplements. These medications may alter the safety and effectiveness of Folic Acid.

Missed dose

If you miss a dose of Folitas, take it as soon as you remember. But if the next dose is near, skip the missed dose and resume the regular dosing regime.

Overdose

If, by accident, you take an overdose of Folic Acid, you may experience nausea, abdominal distension and flatulence. Consult your doctor if you experience these side effects. Strict adherence to the prescribed dose is essential.

Storage

Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat. Keep out of the reach of children and pets.
