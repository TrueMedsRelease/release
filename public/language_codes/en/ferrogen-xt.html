Common use

Ferrogen xt is commonly used to treat iron deficiency and anaemia, including conditions like menorrhagia (heavy menstrual bleeding) and folate deficiency anaemia. Iron helps boost red blood cell production, while folic acid supports overall blood health. Folic acid is also important during pregnancy, as it helps reduce the risk of neural tube defects in the developing fetus. Additionally, this combination is beneficial for people with poor diets, digestive issues, or conditions that affect iron absorption, helping improve energy levels and overall health by preventing or treating iron and folate deficiencies.

Dosage and direction

Adhere strictly to the dosage instructions provided by your doctor. Taking the supplement at a consistent time each day can help maintain stable levels. The tablet should be swallowed whole with water. While often recommended on an empty stomach for better absorption, taking it with food can help minimize side effects like nausea or constipation.

Precautions

Ferrogen xt is safe to take during pregnancy and lactation. However, consult your doctor before taking it in such situations.
People with kidney and liver issues should exercise caution while taking this medication.

Contraindications

Ferrogen xt should be avoided in individuals with allergy to iron, particularly those with bacterial infections. Furthermore, it is contraindicated in patients diagnosed with hemosiderosis, hemochromatosis, and hemolytic anaemia.

Possible side effect

Side effects of Ferrogen xt: Nausea, Dark-Coloured Stool, Constipation, Vomiting.

Drug interaction

Magnesium trisilicate and antacids containing carbonates impede the absorption of iron. Therefore, it is advisable to avoid taking oral iron products simultaneously with oral tetracycline antibiotics, as they can interfere with each other's absorption. It is recommended to separate the intake of these products by at least two hours.

Missed dose

If you forget to take this medicine, take it as soon as you remember. But avoid repeating the dose.

Overdose

Overdose of this medication may cause symptoms like gastrointestinal mucosa: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, epigastric pain, hematemeses and rectorrhagia. If you observe these symptoms, inform your doctor and follow the advice accordingly.

Storage

Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat. Keep out of the reach of children and pets.
