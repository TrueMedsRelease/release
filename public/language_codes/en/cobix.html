Common use

Cobix capsule is a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to relieve pain and inflammation. It's effective for conditions like osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, and menstrual cramps. In addition to its primary use, this medicine also helps to relieve symptoms related to osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, and menstrual cramps. It is particularly beneficial in managing acute pain in adults and treating primary dysmenorrhoea.

Dosage and direction

Swallow the capsule whole with a glass of water. Avoid crushing or chewing it. Remember, if you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember. But never take two doses together to make up for a missed one. Always consult your doctor for more specific advice on how to use this medicine.

Precautions

Cobix should only be used during pregnancy or while breastfeeding if clearly needed and if the benefits outweigh the risks. Always consult your doctor before taking this medication if you are pregnant, planning a pregnancy, or breastfeeding.
Patients with heart disease, liver disease, kidney disease, or a history of stomach ulcers or bleeding should use Cobix with caution.

Contraindications

Cobix should not be taken by patients with a known allergy to celecoxib or to any other component of this medicine. Patients with a history of asthma, urticaria, or other allergic-type reactions after taking aspirin or other NSAIDs should not take Cobix. This medicine is also contraindicated in patients undergoing coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery.

Possible side effect

Cobix capsule may have the following common side effects: Dyspepsia (indigestion, which might cause bloating or discomfort in your tummy); Diarrhea; Abdominal pain; Flatulence; Peripheral edema (this refers to swelling in your hands and feet caused by fluid build-up); Rash.

Drug interaction

Cobix may interact with anticoagulants (such as warfarin), ACE inhibitors (like lisinopril or enalapril), angiotensin receptor blockers (ARBs) (such as losartan or valsartan), diuretics (like furosemide or hydrochlorothiazide) and cancer treatment medicines (like methotrexate).

Missed dose

If you forget to take Cobix capsule at the scheduled time, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it's nearly time for your next dose, skip the missed one and carry on with your regular schedule. Remember, never alter your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

If you accidentally take more than the recommended dose of Cobix capsule, you might feel drowsy or nauseous. In such a case, rinse your mouth with water and rest. If these symptoms persist or worsen, please inform your doctor.

Storage

Store Cobix capsule at room temperature. Keep it away from excessive moisture; a dry place is best. Always keep this medicine out of the reach of children.
