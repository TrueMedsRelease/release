Common use

Lonitab primarily manages hypertension, more commonly termed high blood pressure. This medicine belongs to the antihypertensives group, specifically the vasodilators subgroup. In addition to its main use, this tablet is also indicated for severe hypertension cases associated with damage to organs. It is usually prescribed as a second-line treatment when other antihypertensive therapies have not been effective in controlling the patient's high blood pressure.

Dosage and direction

Consume the tablet orally, with or without food. Swallow it whole, accompanied by a glass of water. Do not crush, chew, or break the tablet. Make sure you take this medicine at the same time each day for best results. For dosage instructions specific to your condition, consult your doctor.

Precautions

Pregnancy: Lonitab should be used by pregnant women only if clearly needed and the benefits outweigh the risks. It is best to consult your doctor before using this medicine during pregnancy.
Breastfeeding: Minoxidil, the active ingredient in Lonitab tablet, can pass into breast milk. If you're nursing, it's wise to consult your doctor before using this medicine.
Liver: If you have any liver health issues, you should use Lonitab with caution since high blood pressure itself can affect liver function. Your doctor will guide you based on your health status.
Kidney: Caution is advised before taking Lonitab tablet if you have a history of kidney problems. It's a good idea to consult your doctor before using this medicine.

Contraindications

Heart Problems: If you have heart conditions, using Lonitab tablet might exacerbate symptoms or induce heart-related complications.
Pheochromocytoma: Patients with this rare adrenal gland tumour should avoid using Lonitab.
Allergy: If you are allergic to minoxidil or any other ingredients in Lonitab, avoid using this medicine as it can lead to allergic reactions.
Children: Lonitab tablet is intended for adult use only.

Possible side effect

Lonitab may have the following common side effects: Swelling; Increased heart rate (tachycardia); Headache; Excessive hair growth; Weight gain; Breast tenderness; Rash or itching; Nausea and vomiting.

Drug interaction

Antihypertensive Medicines: Using alongside other blood pressure-lowering medicines like atenolol and amlodipine may intensify the effect, causing a drastic drop in blood pressure.
Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs (NSAIDs): Medicines like ibuprofen and naproxen may reduce the effectiveness of Lonitab.
Oral Contraceptives: Lonitab tablet may decrease the effectiveness of birth control pills.

Missed dose

In case you forget to take your scheduled dose of Lonitab, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it's nearly time for your next dose, skip the missed one and continue with your standard dosing schedule. Avoid increasing your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

In case you take more than the recommended dose of Lonitab, you might experience symptoms like a rapid heartbeat, fainting, or dizziness. If these symptoms persist, inform your doctor.

Storage

Keep Lonitab in a dry place, with a temperature not exceeding 30 C. Make sure to protect it from light. Don't expose the medicine to moisture. Always ensure that this medication is stored away from children's reach. Remember to use before its expiry date.
