Common use

The primary use of TriOmega is to support cardiovascular health, brain development and function, and eye health. They are essential omega-3 fatty acids found predominantly in fish oil and certain types of fish. Secondary uses include the management of inflammations such as rheumatoid arthritis, inflammatory bowel disease, and asthma due to their anti-inflammatory properties. They also support healthy infant development during pregnancy and breastfeeding.

Dosage and direction

One softgel daily or take as directed by your doctor.

Precautions

It is generally considered safe to use TriOmega during pregnancy and breastfeeding due to its benefits in fetal and infant brain development. However, it is always recommended to consult a doctor before initiating any supplementation during these periods.
Individuals with bleeding disorders or those undergoing treatment with blood thinners should exercise caution when using TriOmega due to its potential effects on blood clotting.
Those scheduled for surgery should inform their doctor about their use of omega-3 supplements due to potential bleeding risks.

Contraindications

TriOmega is contraindicated in individuals with allergies to fish or shellfish, owing to its common derivation from these sources.

Possible side effect

Side effects of TriOmega: Fishy aftertaste or burps, Digestive discomfort or indigestion, Nausea, Diarrhea, Rash (in rare cases).

Drug interaction

TriOmega may interact with anticoagulants (such as warfarin and heparin), blood pressure medications (like amlodipine, lisinopril), and diabetes medications (including metformin and insulin). It is advisable to consult a doctor before starting any new medications to ensure there are no adverse interactions with TriOmega (Docosahexaenoic Acid + Eicosapentaenoic Acid).

Missed dose

If you miss a dose of this medicine, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it is nearly time for the next dose, skip the missed dose and continue with your regular dosing schedule. Do not take a double dose to make up for a forgotten dose.

Overdose

An overdose of TriOmega might lead to increased bleeding risk, especially if taken with other anticoagulants. If suspected, one should seek immediate medical attention.

Storage

Store in a cool, dry and dark place. Keep out of the reach of children and pets.
