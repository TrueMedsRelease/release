Common use

Tynept tablets is an antidepressant that contains dibenzoxazepine. It's a drug that's used to treat depression. It also improves and brightens one's mood, and it's thought to be beneficial in treating depression that hasn't responded to other treatments. Tynept tablets works by boosting the amount of chemical messengers in the brain, which helps to regulate and improve mood.

Dosage and direction

Because it can make you drowsy, it's best to take it before night. It can be taken with or without food, but for best results, take it at the same time every day. Take it all in at once. It should not be chewed, crushed, or broken. Your doctor will determine the dose and duration so that you get the proper amount to control your symptoms.

Precautions

Tynept is not recommended for use during pregnancy or while breastfeeding. It is essential to consult your doctor before using this medicine if you are pregnant or breastfeeding.
Tynept should be used with caution in patients with respiratory conditions due to the risk of respiratory depression.
It should also be used with caution in patients with a history of substance use disorders due to the risk of dependence and withdrawal symptoms.

Contraindications

Tynept is contraindicated in individuals with previous allergic reactions to tianeptine or dibenzoxazepine.
It is also contraindicated in patients with severe renal failure or hepatic insufficiency.

Possible side effect

Side Effects of Tynept tablets: Increased heart rate; Dryness in mouth; Orthostatic hypotension (sudden lowering of blood pressure on standing); Difficulty in urination; Weight gain.

Drug interaction

Tynept may interact with antidepressants (such as Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs), tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs)), anti-anxiety medicines (like benzodiazepines) and blood thinners (like warfarin).

Missed dose

If you have forgotten to take a dose of Tynept, take it as soon as you remember. However, avoid doubling the dose if it is near the time for your next dosage.

Overdose

Overdosing on Tynept could lead to severe symptoms like nausea, vomiting, drowsiness, confusion, and even seizures. In such cases, immediate medical attention should be sought.

Storage

Store Tynept at room temperature, away from moisture and heat. Keep away from children.
