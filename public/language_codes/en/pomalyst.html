Common use

Pomalyst is used to treat multiple myeloma (cancer resulting from a progressive blood disease). It is usually given after at least two other medications have been tried without success. Pomalyst affects the immune system. It promotes immune responses to help slow tumor growth. Pomalyst is also used to treat Kaposi sarcoma (KS) in adults.

Dosage and direction

Take Pomalyst exactly as prescribed by your doctor. Take each dose with a full glass of water. Take the medicine at the same time each day, with or without food. Swallow the capsule whole. Do not break, chew, or open a Pomalyst capsule.

Precautions

To make sure Pomalyst is safe for you, tell your doctor if you have ever had: liver disease (especially hepatitis B); kidney disease (or if you are on dialysis); risk factors for coronary artery disease (such as high blood pressure, high cholesterol, diabetes, menopause, smoking, a family history of coronary artery disease, being overweight, or being older than 40 and a man); if you also use pembrolizumab (Keytruda); or if you smoke (smoking may make pomalidomide less effective and may increase your risk of a stroke or blood clot while taking this medicine).

Contraindications

You should not use Pomalyst if you are allergic to pomalidomide, thalidomide, or you are pregnant. Pomalidomide can cause severe, life-threatening birth defects or death of a baby if the mother or father is taking this medicine at the time of conception or during pregnancy. Even one dose of Pomalyst can cause major defects of the baby's arms and legs, bones, ears, eyes, face, and heart. You should not breastfeed while using this medicine.
For women (if you have not had a hysterectomy): Do not use Pomalyst if you are pregnant. Use two forms of birth control beginning 4 weeks before you start taking Pomalyst and ending 4 weeks after your last dose. For men: If a man fathers a baby while using pomalidomide, the baby may have birth defects. Use a condom to prevent pregnancy while taking Pomalyst, and for up to 4 weeks after your last dose.

Possible side effect

Common Pomalyst side effects may include: low blood cell counts; abnormal lab tests; rash; fever, weakness or feeling tired; nausea, diarrhea, constipation; cold symptoms such as stuffy nose, sneezing, sore throat; back pain; or feeling short of breath.

Drug interaction

Taking Pomalyst with other drugs that cause dizziness or confusion can worsen these effects. Ask your doctor before using opioid medication, a sleeping pill, a muscle relaxer, or medicine for anxiety or seizures.

Missed dose

Use the medicine as soon as you can, but skip the missed dose if you are more than 12 hours late for the dose. Do not use two doses at one time.

Overdose

Seek emergency medical attention.

Storage

Store at room temperature away from moisture and heat. Keep away from children and pets.
