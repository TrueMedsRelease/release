Common use

Natrise tablet is used to treat low sodium levels in the blood (hyponatremia) associated with conditions like heart failure, cirrhosis, and the syndrome of inappropriate antidiuretic hormone (SIADH). It works by blocking the hormone vasopressin, which helps the kidneys retain water, thereby increasing blood sodium levels. This medicine is particularly useful for individuals with conditions like heart failure, liver disease (cirrhosis), and hormonal imbalances such as the Syndrome of Inappropriate Antidiuretic Hormone (SIADH). By regulating sodium levels, Natrise Tablet improves overall health and helps prevent potential complications associated with low sodium.

Dosage and direction

Take Natrise tablet exactly as prescribed by your doctor. You can take it with or without food, but it's recommended to take it at the same time every day. Swallow the tablet whole without chewing or crushing it.

Precautions

Natrise is not recommended for use during pregnancy or while breastfeeding. Consult your doctor before using this medicine if you are pregnant or nursing.
Patients with liver diseases, including cirrhosis, should exercise caution when taking Natrise, as it can cause serious liver injury.

Contraindications

If you have a known allergy to Natrise tablet or any of its ingredients, do not use it. Consult your doctor for alternative options.
Natrise should not be taken by individuals who have an urgent need to raise serum sodium levels acutely. It is also contraindicated in patients who have a lack of ability to sense or respond to thirst. The concomitant use of Natrise with strong CYP 3A inhibitors is contraindicated.

Possible side effect

Side Effects of Natrise: Frequent urge to urinate (You may feel the need to urinate more often than usual); Increased urge to urinate at night (nocturia) - You may wake up multiple times during the night to use the bathroom; Dry mouth (Your mouth may feel dry and parched); Increased thirst (You may feel more thirsty than usual); Constipation (You may experience difficulty in passing stools).

Drug interaction

Diuretics: Furosemide, Hydrochlorothiazide; Antidepressants: Sertraline, Fluoxetine; Antiarrhythmics: Amiodarone, Sotalol; Antifungals: Ketoconazole, Fluconazole; Antibiotics: Ciprofloxacin, Levofloxacin.

Missed dose

Follow the doctor's prescription and never increase your dosage without consulting a doctor.

Overdose

If you accidentally take too much Natrise, seek immediate medical attention.

Storage

Store Natrise tablets in a cool, dry place. Protect it from light and moisture. Keep it out of reach and sight of children. Use the product before the expiry date mentioned on the packaging.
