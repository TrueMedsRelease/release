Common use

Fucidin cream is an antibiotic used to treat cases of bacterial infections of the skin including cellulitis, impetigo, infected eczema, infected dermatitis, and infected wounds, cuts, abrasions or burns.

Dosage and direction

Wash your hands thoroughly with soap or handwash and make sure that the infected area is clean and dry. Take the prescribed amount of the antibiotic cream onto your fingertip and gently rub it to form a thin layer over the infected area. After application, wash your hands properly and leave the treated area uncovered for a few minutes to allow the medication to be absorbed. Follow your doctor's instructions about applying Fucidin cream or refer to the product label for proper guidance on the matter.

Precautions

The safety and potential risks of using Fucidin cream during pregnancy have not been determined. Women who are breastfeeding must exercise caution when it comes to Fucidin cream uses. Talk to your doctor about whether this cream is safe for you and your infant. If the doctor declares it safe for use, then only do it.

Contraindications

Read the ingredient label of this product carefully to spot any potential allergens in your case. If found, avoid using this cream. If you experience any allergic symptoms after applying Fucidin cream, wipe off the cream immediately and call your doctor for guidance and help.

Possible side effect

Irritation at the site of application is the most common side effect of Fucidin cream.

Drug interaction

No specific interactions of Fucidin cream with any other drug have been established yet. Inform your doctor about any medication you are taking to prevent any undesirable interactions.

Missed dose

If you forget to apply Fucidin cream at the scheduled time, do so if you remember it within a few hours. However, if the time for your next dose is close, skip this one and follow your predetermined schedule.

Overdose

Over-application of Fucidin cream can further irritate your skin and may worsen the symptoms of infection. It could lead to dry and flaky skin, redness, itching, and a burning sensation at the application site. If these overdose symptoms get worrying or severe, immediately consult with your doctor for guidance.

Storage

Keep this cream at a slightly cool temperature and away from excess moisture or sunlight. Place this antibiotic cream in a spot where children and pets cannot reach it.
