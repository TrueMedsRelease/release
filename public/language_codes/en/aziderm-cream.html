Common use

Aziderm Cream belongs to the group of dicarboxylic acids. Its application is intended for treating common acne, specifically acne vulgaris. It is also used in the treatment of mild to moderate papulopustular rosacea (spots).

Dosage and direction

Clean and dry the affected area thoroughly before applying the cream. Remember to wash your hands after application unless the affected area is on your hands.

Precautions

Before applying Aziderm Cream, notify your doctor if you have asthma, as it could potentially exacerbate the condition.
Notify your doctor if you are pregnant or planning to conceive before using this medication. Your doctor will determine whether Aziderm Cream is suitable for you.
Exercise caution when using Aziderm Cream while breastfeeding. Consult your doctor before applying it. Ensure that treated skin or breasts do not come into contact with infants.

Contraindications

If you are allergic to Azelaic acid or any other components of this medication, refrain from using Aziderm Cream.

Possible side effect

The common side effects of Aziderm Cream include: Irritation at the site of application; Itching; Redness; Burning sensation at the site of application.

Drug interaction

Ensure that you inform your doctor about any other skin preparations or complementary medications that you are using on the affected area. Also, inform your doctor if you are taking or plan to take nutritional supplements or herbal products before using this cream.

Missed dose

Skipping an Aziderm Cream application may decrease its effectiveness. If you forget to apply it, do so as soon as possible.

Overdose

Adhere to your doctor's prescription for the daily Aziderm Cream usage. It is crucial to stick to the prescribed dosage and avoid surpassing it without consulting your doctor.
