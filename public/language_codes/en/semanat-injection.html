Common use

Semanat injection is a once-weekly medication used to manage type 2 diabetes, support chronic weight management, and reduce cardiovascular risks. It helps improve blood sugar control and aids weight loss in obese or overweight adults with weight-related conditions. It works by mimicking a hormone to regulate appetite and digestion.

Dosage and direction

Inspect the liquid in the pen to ensure it is clear and colorless. Wash hands and sanitize the chosen injection site with an alcohol pad.
Attach a new, sterile needle to the prefilled pen. Pinch a fold of skin at the injection site. Insert the needle into the skin. Press the injection button until the dose counter indicates the full dose has been delivered. Hold the needle in place for several seconds to ensure the full dose is administered. Remove the needle and dispose of it immediately in a designated sharps container.
Rotate injection sites weekly (e.g., using different areas of the abdomen) to prevent skin irritation or lipodystrophy.

Precautions

Semanat injection is a weekly subcutaneous, self-administered medication requiring strict adherence to safety protocols. It must be injected into the abdomen, thigh, or upper arm. Key precautions include never sharing pens or needles, rotating injection sites to prevent skin damage, and avoiding intravenous or intramuscular administration.

Contraindications

Semanat injection (semaglutide) is contraindicated in individuals with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC) or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2), and those with a known allergy to semaglutide.
It should not be used during pregnancy, while breastfeeding, or by patients with severe, active gallbladder or pancreatic disease; patients with severe gastroparesis (slow stomach emptying) or other severe digestive issues.

Possible side effect

Semanat injection commonly causes gastrointestinal side effects, including nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, and abdominal pain, particularly when starting treatment. These issues are usually mild to moderate, temporary, and often subside as the body adjusts to the medication.

Drug interaction

Semanat injections (Semaglutide) primarily interact with other diabetes medications, significantly increasing hypoglycemia risk, and may alter the absorption of oral drugs by delaying gastric emptying. Key interactions include insulin, sulfonylureas, and medications requiring rapid absorption. Close monitoring for hypoglycemia and gastrointestinal symptoms is essential.

Missed dose

Take the medicine as soon as you can, but skip the missed dose if it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take two doses at one time.

Overdose

Taking an overdose of Semanat Injection can lead to severe gastrointestinal distress and potentially life-threatening complications like extreme dehydration or pancreatitis. If an overdose is suspected, contact emergency services immediately.

Storage

Before first use store in the refrigerator at temperatures between 2C to 8C, keep it in the original carton to protect it from light. Never freeze Semanat Injection, as freezing can damage the medication. Once in use, the pen or vial can typically be stored in the refrigerator (2C to 8C) or at controlled room temperature (15C to 30C). Keep out of reach of children and pets.
