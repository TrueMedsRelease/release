Common use

Dutanol is a prescription medicine primarily used to manage benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) in adult men. BPH is a condition where the prostate gland becomes enlarged, which can cause urinary problems. This medicine belongs to a class of medications known as 5-alpha reductase inhibitors. This medicine also improves urinary symptoms associated with this condition. It decreases the risk of acute urinary retention, which is the inability to pass urine, and lessens the chance of needing prostate surgery.

Dosage and direction

Swallow the tablet whole with a glass of water. Avoid crushing or chewing it. Do not take more than your doctor has advised. Remember, it's important not to stop taking this medicine without consulting your doctor first.

Precautions

Liver disease: People with severe liver impairment should exercise caution as Dutanol could affect their condition.
Prostate cancer: Dutasteride may increase the risk of developing high-grade prostate cancer, so caution should be exercised when using Dutanol tablet.
Male breast cancer: There is a possible association between the use of Dutanol and an increased risk of male breast cancer.

Contraindications

Dutanol tablet is contraindicated during pregnancy. If you are pregnant or planning to conceive, make sure to discuss this with your doctor before starting this medicine.
Dutanol tablet is contraindicated for breastfeeding women. It's crucial to consult your doctor to understand the potential risks and benefits.
If you have a known allergy to dutasteride or any other components of Dutanol, it is contraindicated for you.
Dutanol tablet should not be given to children under 18 years of age due to potential health risks.

Possible side effect

While taking Dutanol, you may experience the following side effects: Erectile dysfunction; Decreased libido - This is a lowered interest or desire in sexual activities; Ejaculation disorders - These can be difficulties in ejaculating or changes in the amount of semen; Breast enlargement or tenderness - This could mean a slight increase in the size of your breasts or discomfort when touched; Dizziness.

Drug interaction

5-alpha reductase inhibitors: Medicines like finasteride may interact with Dutanol, potentially affecting its efficacy in reducing DHT levels.
CYP3A4 inhibitors: Medicines such as ketoconazole and ritonavir can increase the concentration of dutasteride in the body when taken with Dutanol.
Alpha-blockers: When combined with Dutanol, medicines like tamsulosin or doxazosin may cause increased side effects, such as dizziness.

Missed dose

If you forget to take your Dutanol tablet, take it as soon as you remember unless it's nearly time for your next dose. If so, just skip the missed dose.

Overdose

If you accidentally take too many Dutanol tablets, you might experience common side effects more intensely. If you notice any increase in side effects, notify your doctor promptly.

Storage

Keep Dutanol in its original packaging until usage. Store in a cool, dry place. Avoid exposure to excessive light and moisture for the medicine. Ensure the medication is out of children's reach and sight. Use this medicine before its expiry date.
