Common use

Eliquis tablet is an anticoagulant used to prevent and treat blood clots, reducing the risk of stroke and systemic embolism in patients with nonvalvular atrial fibrillation. It also helps prevent clots in conditions like deep vein thrombosis and pulmonary embolism by inhibiting clot formation.

Dosage and direction

Here are the recommended steps to take Eliquis tablet:
Swallow the tablet whole with sufficient water.
Do not crush or chew it as this may change how the medicine works.
Always take this medicine exactly as your doctor has directed.
Do not adjust the dose or frequency without consulting your doctor.
Remember, it is crucial not to suddenly stop taking this medicine without discussing it with your doctor first.

Precautions

Caution is advised while taking Eliquis if you are pregnant. It is best to consult your doctor before taking this medicine.
Eliquis should be used with caution if you are breastfeeding. Discuss with your doctor to understand the potential benefits and risks.
The interaction of Eliquis with alcohol is unknown. It's wise to consult your doctor before consuming alcohol while on this medication.
Caution is advised while taking Eliquis in case of liver impairment. Discuss your liver health with your doctor before starting this medicine, as it may affect liver function.
If you have a history of kidney problems, use Eliquis cautiously. It's always a good idea to consult your doctor before starting this medication.
Eliquis may cause dizziness or fatigue. If you experience such symptoms, avoid driving or operating heavy machinery until you feel comfortable doing so.

Contraindications

Eliquis tablet interacts with the following diseases:
Renal Impairment: People with severe kidney disease may need a lower dose due to impaired drug excretion.
Hepatic Impairment: People with liver disease should be cautious as their condition could affect how well Eliquis works.
Active Pathological Bleeding: Eliquis increases the risk of bleeding, so is not recommended for people who are already experiencing abnormal bleeding.
Allergy: If you have ever had a severe hypersensitivity reaction to apixaban, the active ingredient in Eliquis tablet, avoid using this medication. Consult your doctor for alternatives.

This medicine is not recommended for children under 18 years of age. Always consult your doctor before giving any medicine to a child.

Possible side effect

Eliquis may cause side effects like nosebleeds, blood in urine, gastrointestinal bleeding, hematomas, and anemia, leading to fatigue or weakness.
Most side effects are temporary and generally harmless, which resolve on discontinuing the use of Eliquis. However, if you experience any serious side effects or worsening of any of the symptoms, please consult your doctor.

Drug interaction

Eliquis interacts with the following medicines:
Strong dual inhibitors of CYP3A4 and P-gp: Medications like ketoconazole, itraconazole, and ritonavir can increase blood levels of apixaban in Eliquis tablet, potentially increasing your risk of bleeding.
Strong dual inducers of CYP3A4 and P-gp: Medications such as rifampin, carbamazepine, and phenytoin can decrease the effectiveness of Eliquis by reducing apixaban exposure.
Other anticoagulants: Medicines that impair hemostasis like aspirin, NSAIDs, heparin, thrombolytic agents and SSRIs/SNRIs can enhance the anticoagulant effect of Eliquis tablet, possibly increasing your risk of bleeding.

Missed dose

In case you forget to take Eliquis at the scheduled time, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it’s close to the time for the next dose, skip the missed one and continue with your regular schedule. Do not increase your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

Overdosing on Eliquis may lead to more intense side effects such as unusual bleeding or bruising. If you suspect an overdose, do not panic but ensure you inform your doctor about it.

Storage

Store Eliquis in a dry place below 30°C. Avoid exposure to direct sunlight and excessive heat. Keep it sealed in its original packaging to protect it from moisture. Always keep this medicine out of reach of children. Remember to use it before the expiry date printed on the packaging.
