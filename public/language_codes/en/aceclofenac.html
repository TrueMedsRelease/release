Common use

Aceclofenac is a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to relieve pain and reduce inflammation in conditions like rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, and ankylosing spondylitis. It is highly effective for managing joint pain, stiffness, and chronic musculoskeletal conditions, acting by reducing prostaglandins that cause inflammation.

Dosage and direction

Aceclofenac is generally taken orally, swallowed whole with water during or after food to minimize stomach irritation. It is advised not to crush or chew tablets. Using the lowest effective dose for the shortest duration is recommended to minimize potential side effects.

Precautions

Requiring caution to avoid serious gastrointestinal (bleeding, ulcers), cardiovascular (heart attack, stroke), and renal (kidney failure) side effects. Use the lowest effective dose for the shortest duration, avoid if you have active ulcers, severe kidney or liver disease, or are in the last trimester of pregnancy.

Contraindications

Avoid with established heart or cerebrovascular disease. Avoid if you have severe kidney or liver disease. Monitor renal function closely if you have mild-to-moderate impairment. Avoid, particularly in the third trimester as it can cause fetal harm. Do not take if you have a known allergy to aceclofenac, aspirin, or other NSAIDs.

Possible side effect

Aceclofenac is commonly causing gastrointestinal issues (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, indigestion, abdominal pain) and dizziness.

Drug interaction

Aceclofenac is an NSAID that significantly interacts with blood thinners (warfarin), other NSAIDs (aspirin, ibuprofen), diuretics, and antihypertensives (ACE inhibitors/ARBs), increasing risks of bleeding, gastrointestinal ulceration, acute renal failure, and kidney toxicity.

Missed dose

Take the medicine as soon as you can, but skip the missed dose if it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take two doses at one time.

Overdose

If an overdose is suspected, contact emergency services immediately.

Storage

Store at room temperature away from moisture and heat. Keep out of reach of the children.
