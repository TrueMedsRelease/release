Common use

Omnacortil tablet is used to treat severe allergic reactions, skin disorders, eye infections, and respiratory conditions like asthma. By suppressing immune responses and reducing inflammation, it effectively relieves symptoms such as redness, swelling, and airway irritation. For optimal results, follow your doctor's prescription.
This medicine can effectively manage problems in different organs like the bones, joints, skin, kidneys, and lungs. In certain cases, it can also be used alongside appropriate chemotherapy to manage specific infections. This medicine contains an active ingredient called prednisolone.

Dosage and direction

Take Omnacortil as prescribed by your doctor. Swallow the tablet whole with water at the same time(s) every day. To avoid an upset stomach, take it with food. Consult your doctor for the specific dosage instructions tailored to your condition. Do not stop taking the medicine suddenly; follow the complete duration of treatment prescribed by your doctor.

Precautions

If you are pregnant, it is important to consult your doctor before using Omnacortil. They will assess the benefits and risks for you and your baby.
It is advised to avoid consuming alcohol while taking Omnacortil as it may increase the risk of certain side effects.
If you have liver disease, use Omnacortil with caution and inform your doctor about your condition.
Omnacortil is safe to use in patients with kidney disease. However, it is important to inform your doctor if you have any kidney issues.
If you have known allergies or hypersensitivity to prednisolone or any other ingredients in Omnacortil, do not use this medicine.

Contraindications

Infections: Omnacortil can suppress the immune system, making it harder for the body to fight infections.
Diabetes: Omnacortil may increase blood sugar levels and require adjustments in antidiabetic medication dosage.
Osteoporosis: Long-term use of Omnacortil may increase the risk of osteoporosis and fractures.
Hypertension: Omnacortil may cause fluid retention and worsen high blood pressure.
Peptic ulcer disease: Omnacortil may increase the risk of gastrointestinal bleeding and ulcers.

Possible side effect

Here are a few common side effects of Omnacortil tablet:
Increased appetite: You may feel hungrier than usual while taking Omnacortil. This can lead to weight gain if you're not careful.
Upset stomach: Some people may experience indigestion or discomfort in the stomach after taking Omnacortil. Taking medicine with food can help avoid this.
Mood changes: Omnacortil can sometimes affect your mood and make you feel irritable or restless.
Weight gain: Maintain a balanced diet and engage in regular physical activity to manage your weight.
Difficulty sleeping: Establish a bedtime routine and create a calm environment in your bedroom to promote better sleep.
Fluid retention: You might retain more water, leading to swelling in certain areas of your body.

Drug interaction

Certain drugs can interact with Omnacortil, potentially affecting its effectiveness or increasing the risk of side effects.
Anticoagulants: Warfarin.
Antifungal medications: Ketoconazole, Fluconazole.
Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs): Ibuprofen, Naproxen.
Antidiabetic medications: Insulin, Metformin.
Immunosuppressants: Cyclosporine, Tacrolimus.

Missed dose

If you forget a dose, take it as soon as you remember. If it's almost time for the next dose, skip the missed dose and continue with your regular schedule.

Overdose

Taking too much Omnacortil can lead to overdose symptoms such as increased blood pressure, weight gain, and fluid retention. If you accidentally take an excessive amount, seek immediate medical attention by contacting your doctor or visiting a nearby hospital.

Storage

Store Omnacortil in a cool and dry place. Protect the medicine from light and moisture by keeping it in its original packaging. Keep Omnacortil out of reach and sight of children to prevent accidental ingestion. Use the medicine before the expiry date mentioned on the packaging.
