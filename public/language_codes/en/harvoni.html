Common use

Harvoni is used to treat hepatitis C virus (HCV) infections in adults and children aged 3 years and older. Harvoni is used for HCV genotypes 1, 4, 5, or 6 infections without cirrhosis or with compensated cirrhosis. Harvoni also treats HCV genotype 1 infection with advanced cirrhosis (decompensated) in combination with ribavirin and people with HCV genotype 1 or 4 infection without cirrhosis or with compensated cirrhosis who have had a liver transplant, in combination with ribavirin.

Dosage and direction

Take Harvoni treatment exactly as your healthcare provider tells you to take it. Do not change your dose unless your healthcare provider tells you to. Take Harvoni tablets by mouth, with or without food. You must not miss or skip doses during treatment. The usual dosage of Harvoni to treat HCV in adults is Harvoni 90mg + 400mg, 1 tablet each day. Harvoni is usually given for 12 weeks.

Precautions

Before taking Harvoni, tell your healthcare provider about all of your medical conditions, including if you: have ever had a hepatitis B virus infection; have liver problems other than hepatitis C infection; have had a liver transplant; have kidney problems or you are on dialysis; have HIV infection; are pregnant or plan to become pregnant; are breastfeeding or plan to breastfeed.
It is not known if Harvoni will harm your unborn baby. It is not known if Harvoni passes into your breast milk. Talk to your healthcare provider about the best way to feed your baby during treatment with Harvoni.

Contraindications

Harvoni is contraindicated in patients with hypersensitivity to its active substances: Ledipasvir and Sofosbuvir, and when used with strong P-gp inducers (e.g., rifampicin, St. John's Wort, carbamazepine) which reduce its efficacy. It should not be used with other sofosbuvir-containing products or rosuvastatin.

Possible side effect

The most common side effects of Harvoni include: tiredness; headache; weakness.

Drug interaction

Tell your healthcare provider if you take: Amiodarone; P-gp inducers and/or moderate to strong CYP inducers (such as rifampin, St. John’s wort, and carbamazepine); Omeprazole (Prilosec) or an antacid.
Clearance of HCV with direct-acting antivirals may lead to changes in liver function, which may impact the safe and effective use of other medications. Frequent laboratory monitoring (INR and blood glucose) and dose adjustments may be necessary.

Missed dose

Do not miss a dose of Harvoni. Missing a dose lowers the amount of medicine in your blood. If you miss a dose of Harvoni, take the medicine as soon as you can, but skip the missed dose if it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take two doses at one time.

Overdose

If you take too much Harvoni, call your healthcare provider or go to the nearest hospital emergency room right away.

Storage

Store Harvoni tablets below 86°F (30°C). Keep them in their original container. Keep out of the reach of children and pets.
