Common use

Sartel tablet is used for managing high blood pressure and heart-related conditions. It helps control your blood pressure and keeps your heart healthy. This medicine is commonly prescribed by doctors to people with hypertension, which is when the force of blood against the walls of your arteries is too high. It can also be used to manage certain heart conditions like heart failure or post-heart attack complications.

Dosage and direction

Consult your doctor for specific advice on dosage and treatment duration. The recommended dosage may vary based on your condition and response to treatment.

Precautions

People with kidney or liver disease may need to take precautions while using Sartel tablet.
If you have diabetes, low blood pressure, or are taking medications like ACE inhibitors or diuretics, consult your doctor before starting Sartel tablet.

Contraindications

If you are pregnant or planning to become pregnant, it is important to know that Sartel tablet should not be taken during the second or third trimester. Consult your doctor for a suitable alternative.
If you are breastfeeding, Sartel tablet is not recommended as it may pass into breast milk and harm the baby. Talk to your doctor about other options.
Do not take Sartel tablet if you are allergic to telmisartan or any other ingredients in the medication. Consult your doctor for alternative options.
This medicine is not recommended for use in children. Please consult a paediatrician for appropriate treatment options.

Possible side effect

Flu-like symptoms: You may experience symptoms such as fever, chills, body aches, and fatigue. These usually go away on their own.
Back pain: Some people may experience discomfort or pain in the back. It is important to rest and avoid strenuous activities until the pain subsides.
Sinus infection: You may develop a sinus infection, which can cause symptoms like facial pain, nasal congestion, and headache. Stay hydrated and use saline nasal sprays to relieve congestion.
Dizziness: You may feel lightheaded or unsteady. To prevent falls, take your time when getting up from a sitting or lying position.

Drug interaction

Some categories of drugs that can interact with Sartel tablet include: ACE inhibitors: Ramipril, Lisinopril; Diuretics: Hydrochlorothiazide, Furosemide; Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs): Ibuprofen, Naproxen.

Missed dose

If you forget to take a dose of Sartel tablet, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it is close to the time for your next scheduled dose, skip the missed one and continue with your regular dosage schedule. Never increase your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

Taking too much Sartel tablet can lead to an overdose, which may cause harmful effects. If you accidentally take more than the prescribed amount, seek immediate medical attention. Contact your doctor or visit a nearby hospital.

Storage

Store Sartel in its original packaging. Protect the medicine from sunlight and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children. Use the product before its expiry date.
