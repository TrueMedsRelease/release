Common use

Placentrex Cream is used for promoting the recovery of wounds and reducing scarring. It is formulated with placental extracts, which are rich in regenerative properties that support skin repair and renewal. The cream contains key components such as glutamic acid, leucine, and lysine, which help enhance collagen production and improve skin elasticity. Its formulation also includes preservatives like methylparaben and propylparaben to ensure product stability. This combination of ingredients helps in soothing irritated skin, reducing inflammation, and maintaining optimal hydration, making it an effective choice for post-surgical care and managing slow-healing injuries.

Dosage and direction

Apply Placentrex Cream directly under hygienic conditions or smear on sterile dressing for application to the affected part, if needed, secure with a bandage or adhesive plaster or as directed by the physician.
Key Uses of Placentrex Cream: Supports Wound Healing; For Scar Reduction; Provides Anti-Inflammatory Action; Helps Hydrate the Skin; For Skin Rejuvenation.

Precautions

Perform a patch test before first use to check for any allergic reactions or sensitivities.
Wash your hands thoroughly before and after applying the cream to reduce the chances of infection and maintain hygiene.
Consult your doctor before using this cream if you are pregnant or planning to become pregnant.

Contraindications

It contains methylparaben and propylparaben, which can cause allergic reactions in some individuals. If you have a known sensitivity to these preservatives or any other ingredients, consult your doctor before use.

Possible side effect

Be aware of any unusual reactions such as redness, swelling, or itching. If these occur, discontinue use and seek medical advice immediately.

Drug interaction

Inform your doctor of any other topical treatments or medications you are using to avoid potential interactions.

Missed dose

If you miss a dose of Placentrex cream, apply it as soon as you remember. However, if it's close to the time for your next dose, skip the missed dose and resume your regular dosing schedule. Don't apply extra cream to make up for the missed dose.

Overdose

Using too much Placentrex cream can cause redness, swelling, or itching and discomfort. If you accidentally apply too much Placentrex cream, simply wash off the excess with mild soap and water. If you experience severe skin irritation, seek medical attention.

Storage

Keep out of the reach of children or pets. Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat.
