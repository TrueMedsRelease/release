Common use

Fertogard is primarily used to manage female infertility. This medicine is part of a group known as selective estrogen receptor modulators (SERMs). This tablet is also used in cases of ovulatory disorders such as polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) or hypothalamic dysfunction. It assists women who are undergoing assisted reproductive technologies (ART) like intrauterine insemination (IUI) or in vitro fertilisation (IVF).

Dosage and direction

Swallow the tablet whole with a glass of water. Do not crush or chew the tablet. It's essential to take this medicine regularly without missing doses. Always follow your doctor's advice on how long to take this medicine.

Precautions

Liver Disease: If you have liver disease, be cautious, as Fertogard could exacerbate your condition.
Abnormal Uterine Bleeding: If you experience abnormal uterine bleeding, using Fertogard could potentially worsen your condition.
Thyroid Disorders: In people with thyroid disorders, this medication may cause a change in hormone levels.
Adrenal Gland Disorders: Those with adrenal gland disorders should exercise care as this medicine may affect hormone production.

Contraindications

Fertogard is contraindicated during pregnancy. If you are pregnant or planning to conceive, make sure to discuss this with your doctor before starting this medicine.
Caution is advised while taking Fertogard if you are breastfeeding. It is best to consult your doctor before taking this medicine.
Do not take Fertogard if you are allergic to clomifene or any other ingredients. Also, make sure to inform your doctor about any allergies you might have.
Fertogard is intended for adult use and should not be given to children under 18 years of age without medical supervision.

Possible side effect

Using Fertogard tablet may result in the following common side effects: Hot flashes; Headache; Nausea and vomiting; Breast discomfort; Abdominal bloating; Mood changes.

Drug interaction

Selective Estrogen Receptor Modulators (SERMs): The effectiveness of Fertogard tablet may be reduced when taken together with tamoxifen.
Antifungal Medications: Fluconazole, a CYP3A4 inhibitor, can increase the levels of clomifene, necessitating careful monitoring for adverse effects.

Missed dose

If you forget to take your Fertogard tablet at the scheduled time, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it's nearly time for your next dose, skip the missed one and continue with your regular schedule. Do not increase your dosage without consulting with your doctor.

Overdose

Overdosing on Fertogard could lead to symptoms similar to its side effects, such as hot flashes, headache, nausea, and abdominal bloating. If you've mistakenly taken more than prescribed and experience any discomfort, inform your doctor promptly.
