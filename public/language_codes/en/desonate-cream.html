Common use

Desonate Cream contains desonide, a corticosteroid used to treat inflammatory skin conditions like atopic dermatitis (eczema), seborrheic dermatitis, and contact dermatitis. It works by blocking chemicals that cause redness, swelling, and itching.

Dosage and direction

Before using the cream, wash your hands properly. Clean and dry the affected area. Put a thin layer of the cream on the infected area, ensuring it covers the entire lesion. Gently rub the cream onto the skin until completely absorbed. Please wash your hands after applying it unless your hands are in the affected area.

Precautions

Desonate Cream should be used during pregnancy and breastfeeding only if the potential benefit justifies the potential risk to the foetus. Consult your doctor before using this medicine during pregnancy and lactation.
Patients with skin infections, rosacea, acne or perioral dermatitis should exercise caution when using Desonate Cream.

Contraindications

This medicine is contraindicated in patients with a known allergy to Desonide or any of its components. It is also contraindicated in patients with infected skin lesions caused by bacteria, fungi, or viruses.

Possible side effect

Desonate Cream may cause the following side effects: erythema (redness of the skin), skin peeling and dryness, burning sensation, irritation, stinging sensation, and itching at the application site.

Drug interaction

Desonate Cream (Desonide) may interact with other topical corticosteroids (e.g., hydrocortisone, prednisone), and immunosuppressants (e.g., tacrolimus, cyclosporin).

Missed dose

If you forget to apply this medicine, apply it as soon as you remember. But avoid applying extra to make up for a missed dose.

Overdose

Overdose of Desonate Cream is unlikely as it is intended for external use only. Using the prescribed dosage is recommended to avoid any side effects.

Storage

Store Desonate Cream at room temperature (15-25ºC). Please protect it from light and moisture. Keep it away from children and pets.
