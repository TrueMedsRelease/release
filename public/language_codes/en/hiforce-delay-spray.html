Common use

Hiforce Delay Spray is a topical anesthetic containing Lidocaine, designed primarily to manage premature ejaculation by temporarily reducing penile sensitivity. It helps prolong sexual intimacy and improve performance by curbing excessive sensitivity. It is also used in medical contexts to numb skin before minor procedures or as an anesthetic lubricant.

Dosage and direction

Hiforce Delay Spray should be applied by spraying 2–5 times onto the penile shaft and glans 10–15 minutes before intercourse to temporarily reduce sensitivity and manage premature ejaculation. For best results, shake well, apply to dry skin, and wipe off any excess before sexual activity to prevent transferring numbness to your partner.

Precautions

Before using it, tell your doctor if you have liver disease or are taking medicines for heart rhythm problems. Although medicines used on the skin are not normally affected by other medicines you should let your doctor know if you are taking any other medicines to be safe.

Contraindications

Do not use Hiforce Delay Spray if you have allergies to local anesthetics or experience skin sensitivity to similar products. Avoid use on infected, irritated, or broken skin.

Possible side effect

The most common side effects of this medicine include redness, rash, burning, and itching at the site of application. These are usually mild and short-lived. Consult your doctor if any of the side effects persist or worry you.

Drug interaction

Hiforce Delay Spray primarily interacts with other local anesthetics, which can lead to increased systemic absorption and potential toxicity. Missed dose

Use only as needed before sexual activity. No routine dosing is required.

Overdose

Over-application may cause excessive numbness or irritation. Seek medical attention if severe discomfort or allergic reaction occurs.

Storage

Store Hiforce Delay Spray at room temperature, away from heat, moisture, and sunlight. Keep the bottle tightly closed and out of reach of children and pets.
