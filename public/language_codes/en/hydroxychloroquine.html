Common use

Hydroxychloroquine is primarily used to manage acute attacks of malaria. This medicine is not a combination medicine but belongs to the group known as antimalarials. In addition to managing malaria, this medicine is also employed for controlling symptoms associated with chronic discoid and systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) and rheumatoid arthritis in adults. It's important to note that it doesn't work against complicated or chloroquine-resistant strains of malaria.

Dosage and direction

Follow the dosage and timing instructions provided by your doctor precisely. Do not change or exceed the prescribed dose without consulting your doctor. Consult your doctor before discontinuing the medication; do not stop taking it without their guidance. Adhere to the dietary recommendations given by your doctor.

Precautions

Psoriasis and Porphyria: This medicine could worsen these conditions.
Liver Disease: Patients with liver issues should use Hydroxychloroquine cautiously as it accumulates in the liver tissue.
Kidney Disease: Since this medicine accumulates in kidney tissue, individuals with kidney conditions should use it judiciously.
Heart Conditions: Because it can affect heart rhythm, those with heart disease should exercise caution while using this medicine.

Contraindications

Do not use Hydroxychloroquine if you have an allergic reaction (hypersensitivity) to any of its components. Do not take this medicine if you consume alcohol. Due to a possible interaction, Hydroxychloroquine may increase the chance of serious adverse effects.
Using Hydroxychloroquine during pregnancy may not be safe. Please consult your physician.

Possible side effect

While taking Hydroxychloroquine, you might experience the following side effects: Nausea; Diarrhea; Abdominal pain; Headache; Itching or Skin Rash; Muscle Weakness; Visual Disturbances.

Drug interaction

It is not recommended to use any anti-cancer medications (tamoxifen), heart medications (digoxin, amiodarone), antidiabetic medications (insulin), antimalarial medications (halofantrine, mefloquine), and antibiotics (moxifloxacin, azithromycin, penicillamine), immunosuppressants (such as ciclosporin), medications for heartburn (such as cimetidine), antipsychotics (such as amisulpride and quetiapine), drugs for fits, and anticoagulants. Before using any prescription or over-the-counter medications, you must consult your doctor.

Missed dose

If you forget to take your regular dose of Hydroxychloroquine, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it's nearly time for your next dose, skip the missed one and continue with your regular schedule. Never adjust your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

An overdose of Hydroxychloroquine can cause symptoms such as headache, drowsiness, and vision changes. If you suspect an overdose, it's important not to panic but inform your doctor right away.
