Common use

Ginette 35 tablet is a medicine used to manage polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), a condition that affects the ovaries and causes symptoms like excessive hair growth, acne, and irregular periods. It helps alleviate common symptoms associated with PCOS. It can reduce unwanted hair growth and acne by blocking the overproduction of certain hormones in the ovaries. Additionally, it helps regulate the menstrual cycle in individuals with PCOS.

Dosage and direction

Take Ginette 35 tablet at the same time every day. You can take it with or without food. Follow the prescribed dose and duration given by your doctor. Complete the full course of treatment as advised by your doctor.

Precautions

Ginette 35 tablet is considered unsafe during pregnancy. Please consult your doctor if you are pregnant or planning to become pregnant.
It is likely unsafe to use Ginette 35 tablet while breastfeeding, as limited data suggests potential harm to the baby. Please seek advice from your doctor.
Exercise caution when consuming alcohol while taking Ginette 35.

Contraindications

Individuals with a history of liver disease should use Ginette 35 tablet with caution and under the guidance of a doctor.
It is important to inform your doctor if you have a history of blood clotting disorders or if you are at an increased risk of developing blood clots.
Individuals with a history of breast cancer or other hormone-sensitive cancers should avoid using Ginette 35 tablet.
If you have known allergies to cyproterone, ethinyl estradiol, or any other ingredients in Ginette 35 tablet, please inform your doctor before using the medicine.

Possible side effect

Taking Ginette 35 may cause some side effects. Here are a few common ones to be aware of: Nausea; Stomach pain; Headache; Weight gain; Breast pain.

Drug interaction

Interactions may occur with the following categories of drugs: Antiepileptic drugs: carbamazepine, phenobarbital; Antifungal drugs: ketoconazole, fluconazole; Antiretroviral drugs: ritonavir, nevirapine; Antibiotics: rifampicin, clarithromycin.

Missed dose

If you forget to take Ginette 35 tablet as instructed, take it as soon as you remember. If it's almost time for your next dose, skip the missed one now and resume your regular dosage schedule. Never increase your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

If you take too much Ginette 35, it can lead to side effects such as nausea, stomach pain, headache, weight gain, breast pain, and mood changes, get immediate medical attention. Contact your doctor or visit a nearby hospital.
