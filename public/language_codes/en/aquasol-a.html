Common use

Aquasol A (25000 IU) capsules is used to manage vitamin A deficiency and related conditions such as night blindness and eye dryness. It helps support normal vision, maintain healthy skin and epithelial tissues, and strengthen immune function, especially in individuals with absorption difficulties.

Dosage and direction

Two to six capsules a day or as directed by the physician. It is recommended to take it with food for better absorption.

Precautions

High intakes of Aquasol A during early pregnancy can cause birth defects. It is advisable to consult your doctor before using this medicine during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
Individuals with liver disease or those with chronic alcohol use should exercise caution when taking high-dose Aquasol A supplements due to the increased risk of toxicity.

Contraindications

Aquasol A supplements are contraindicated in people with known hypersensitivity to retinoids or any component of the formulation.
Avoid excessive intake, as high doses may cause toxicity.

Possible side effect

Side effects of Aquasol A: Nausea, Headache, Blurred vision, Bone pain, Skin dryness or peeling.

Drug interaction

Aquasol A can interact with lipid-lowering agents (such as cholestyramine and colestipol), gastrointestinal lubricants (like mineral oil and magnesium hydroxide), and anti-obesity medications (such as orlistat and phentermine-topiramate).

Missed dose

If you miss a dose of this medicine, take it as soon as you remember. But avoid taking double doses to make up for the missed one.

Overdose

Overdose of Aquasol A may cause symptoms like nausea, headache, blurred vision, bone pain, skin dryness/peeling, and in severe cases lead to liver damage. If you observe any of these symptoms, inform your doctor immediately.

Storage

Store away from heat, moisture, and light. Keep out of reach of children and pets.
