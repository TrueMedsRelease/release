Common use

Tessalon is a non-narcotic cough medicine that numbs the throat and lungs, making the cough reflex less active. Tessalon is used to relieve coughing.

Dosage and direction

Follow all directions on your prescription label and read all medication guides or instruction sheets. Use the medicine exactly as directed. Never suck or chew on a Tessalon capsule. Swallow the pill whole. Sucking or chewing the capsule may cause side effects.

Precautions

Tell your doctor if you are pregnant or breastfeeding.
Tessalon is not approved for use by anyone younger than 10 years old. An overdose of benzonatate can be fatal, especially to a young child who has accidentally swallowed the medicine.

Contraindications

Never suck or chew on a Tessalon capsule. Swallow the pill whole. Sucking or chewing the capsule may cause serious side effects.
You should not use Tessalon if you are allergic to benzonatate or topical numbing medicines such as tetracaine or procaine (found in some insect bite and sunburn creams).

Possible side effect

Common side effects of Tessalon may include: headache, dizziness; nausea, upset stomach; constipation; itching, rash; or stuffy nose.

Drug interaction

Using Tessalon with other drugs that make you drowsy can worsen this effect. Ask your doctor before using opioid medication, a sleeping pill, a muscle relaxer, or medicine for anxiety or seizures.

Missed dose

Skip the missed dose and use your next dose at the regular time. Do not use two doses at one time.

Overdose

Seek emergency medical attention, an overdose of Tessalon can be fatal, especially to a child. Overdose symptoms may include tremors, feeling restless, seizure (convulsions), slow heart rate, weak pulse, fainting, and slow breathing (breathing may stop).

Storage

Store at room temperature away from moisture, heat, and light. Keep away from children and pets.
