Common use

Azee tablet is a broad-spectrum antibiotic used to treat various bacterial infections such as respiratory tract infections, skin infections, ear infections, and sexually transmitted diseases. It works by inhibiting bacterial protein synthesis to stop infection growth. The medication works by hindering the growth of bacteria, helping the body's immune system fight off the infection.

Dosage and direction

Azee tablets should be swallowed whole with a glass of water. Do not crush the tablet, as this may affect the effectiveness of the medication. The dosage of Azee depends on the type and severity of the infection being treated. It is essential to take the medication exactly as the healthcare provider prescribes.

Precautions

The Azee tablet is not recommended for use in pregnant women unless absolutely necessary. Before taking this medicine, the risks and benefits should be discussed with the doctor.
It is known to be secreted into breast milk, and its safety in breastfeeding mothers has not been established. So, consult your doctor before consuming the Azee tablet.
Azee tablets should be used with caution in patients with kidney impairment or impaired liver function.

Contraindications

Azee tablets should not be used in patients with a known allergy to azithromycin or any macrolide antibiotics.
The Azee tablet is not recommended for use in children below 10 years of age.

Possible side effect

The side effects of Azee are: Diarrhea; Headache; Vomiting; Stomach pain; Loss of appetite.

Drug interaction

Azee tablet can interact with other medications including: Domperidone; Pimozide; Amisulpride; Ergotamine; Dihydroergotamine; Simvastatin; Tolterodine; Colchicine.

Missed dose

If you forget to take your dose of Azee tablet, be sure to take it as soon as possible. However, if the time for your next dose is near, skip the missed one and resume with the normal dosing schedule.

Overdose

Azee tablet is a powerful medication and if taken in excess, can result in an overdose. Symptoms of such an occurrence may include diarrhea, nausea, and hearing loss. Seek emergency medical attention.
