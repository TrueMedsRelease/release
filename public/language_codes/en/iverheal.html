Common use

Iverheal tablet is an effective medication primarily used for managing a variety of parasitic infections. This medicine falls under the class of medicines known as ectoparasiticides.
This medicine is also used to manage intestinal strongyloidiasis and onchocerciasis, infections caused by parasitic worms such as Wuchereria bancrofti, and external parasitic infestations like head lice and scabies.

Dosage and direction

Swallow the tablet whole with water. It's best to take it on an empty stomach. The tablet should not be crushed or chewed. Should you have any doubts about the dose or frequency, consult your doctor. Do not stop taking this medicine abruptly without consulting your doctor.

Precautions

Caution is advised while taking Iverheal if you are pregnant. It is best to consult your doctor before taking this medicine. If you are breastfeeding, you should weigh the benefits and risks of using Iverheal, as it may pass into breast milk.
Proceed with caution while taking Iverheal tablet if you regularly consume alcohol. It's always wise to inform your doctor about your drinking habits before beginning a new medication.
If you have liver problems, caution is advised when taking Iverheal. This medicine may affect liver function, so discuss any liver issues with your doctor beforehand.
This medicine should be used with care if you have kidney health issues. Always consult your doctor before starting.
Iverheal tablet may cause dizziness or fatigue in some people. If you experience these side effects, avoid driving or operating heavy machinery until you feel better.
Older patients should take Iverheal tablet under medical supervision, as they may be more susceptible to its side effects.

Contraindications

Liver disease: Caution should be exercised when using Iverheal in patients with impaired liver function, as it may exacerbate their condition.
Allergy: Iverheal tablet is contraindicated in patients who have an allergy to ivermectin or any other ingredients in the tablet. Make sure to inform your doctor about any past allergic reactions.
Children: Iverheal is not meant for use in children under the age of 18 unless medically prescribed. Always consult your doctor before giving this medicine to a child.

Possible side effect

The following are some of the common side effects that you might experience with Iverheal:
Headache; Dizziness; Diarrhoea; Nausea; Vomiting; Skin rashes and itching; Stomach pain; Weakness; Fatigue.

Most side effects are temporary, usually harmless, and resolve upon discontinuing the use of Iverheal tablet. However, if you experience serious side effects or worsening of any symptoms, inform your doctor promptly.

Drug interaction

Iverheal interacts with the following medicines:
Anticoagulants: Blood-thinning medicines like warfarin may interact with Iverheal tablet, which can increase the risk of bleeding.
Benzodiazepines: Use of Iverheal tablet along with benzodiazepines like alprazolam and diazepam may enhance neurotoxicity and respiratory depression.
Barbiturates: Medicines such as phenobarbital and amobarbital can cause significantly increased CNS depression when used in combination with Iverheal.

Missed dose

If you forget to take your regular dose of Iverheal, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it's nearly time for your next dose, skip the missed one and continue with your usual schedule. Do not increase your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

An overdose of Iverheal may lead to mild symptoms such as headache, dizziness, skin rash, and vomiting. If you've taken more than the prescribed dosage and begin feeling unwell, it's important to inform your doctor.
