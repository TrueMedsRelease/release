Common use

Cyklokapron is an antifibrinolytic medication used to prevent or reduce excessive bleeding by stopping the breakdown of blood clots. It is primarily used to treat heavy menstrual periods, bleeding after dental surgery in hemophiliacs, prostate surgery, and hereditary angioedema.

Dosage and direction

For heavy menstrual bleeding, the medication is taken at specific intervals, starting at the onset of heavy bleeding. A healthcare provider will determine the appropriate frequency for your condition. It is typically taken for a limited number of days during the menstrual cycle. Swallow tablets whole with water; do not cut, crush, or chew them. Can be taken with or without food.

Precautions

Tell your doctor if you have ever had: kidney disease; a bladder or kidney infection; or leukemia. Tell your doctor if you are pregnant or breastfeeding.

Contraindications

You should not use Cyklokapron if you are allergic to tranexamic acid, or if you have: problems with the blood vessels in your eyes; color blindness; or a history of stroke, blood clot, or bleeding in your brain.

Possible side effect

Common side effects include: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea; dizziness, feeling light-headed; mild itching or rash; or feeling unusually happy.

Drug interaction

Tell your doctor about all your other medicines, especially: any type of medication to treat a bleeding episode or a blood clot; or factor IX complex (Bebulin, Bebulin VH, Profilnine, and others).

Missed dose

Take the medicine as soon as you can, but skip the missed dose if it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take two doses at one time.

Overdose

Seek emergency medical attention. Overdose symptoms may include headache, confusion, vision problems, vomiting, diarrhea, numbness or weakness, problems with speech or balance, feeling light-headed, or fainting.

Storage

Store this medicine at room temperature. Keep away from children and pets.
