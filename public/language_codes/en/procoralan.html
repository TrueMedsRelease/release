Common use

Procoralan is used to manage chronic stable angina (chest pain) in patients who cannot take beta-blockers or require additional treatment. It is also prescribed for chronic heart failure to reduce the risk of hospitalisation due to worsening symptoms. By lowering heart rate, Ivabradine helps improve blood flow and oxygen supply to the heart, reducing strain on the heart muscle.

Dosage and direction

Before using Procoralan, consult your doctor for personalized guidance on dosage and usage. Swallow the tablet with a glass of water. Do not crush or chew it. The correct dose and duration will be determined by your doctor based on factors such as age, body weight, and condition.

Precautions

Patients with kidney or liver disease, low blood pressure, atrial fibrillation, or a history of stroke should take Procoralan under strict medical supervision. This medication may worsen bradycardia and should be used cautiously in individuals prone to low heart rate.

Contraindications

Procoralan is contraindicated in patients with severe liver disease, very low resting heart rate (below 60 beats per minute), acute heart failure, or unstable angina. It should not be used in patients with severe low blood pressure, pacemaker dependence, or those taking strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (such as ketoconazole or clarithromycin).
Procoralan is not recommended during pregnancy, as it may harm the developing fetus. Women of childbearing age should use effective contraception while taking this medication. It is also not advised during breastfeeding, as it may pass into breast milk and affect the infant.

Possible side effect

Procoralan may cause side effects like increased temporary visual brightness, blurred vision, slow heart rate, low blood pressure, dizziness, headache, and fatigue. If you experience visual disturbances or severe dizziness, seek medical advice immediately.

Drug interaction

Avoid taking beta-blockers (such as propranolol and metoprolol), calcium channel blockers (such as diltiazem and verapamil), antifungal medications (such as itraconazole), macrolide antibiotics (such as erythromycin), and certain heart rhythm medications (such as amiodarone). These medications may increase the risk of bradycardia or cause dangerous interactions.

Missed dose

If a dose is missed, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it is close to the next scheduled dose, skip the missed dose and resume the regular dosing schedule. Do not double the dose to make up for a missed one.

Overdose

An overdose may cause severe bradycardia, dizziness, or fainting. If an overdose is suspected, seek immediate medical attention.

Storage

Store Procoralan at room temperature. Protect it from direct sunlight, moisture, and heat. Keep it in its original packaging. Keep it out of the reach of children and pets.
