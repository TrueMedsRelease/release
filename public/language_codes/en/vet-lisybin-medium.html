Common use

Lisybin Medium is a veterinary medicine used to support, maintain, and protect liver function in medium-sized dogs (around 6-16kg), acting as a hepatoprotective agent that aids liver cell repair and regeneration by boosting glutathione levels. It's used for various liver conditions, improving digestion, appetite, and bile flow.

Dosage and direction

To use Lisybin Medium for your dog (6-16kg), give one tablet daily, with or without food, but always under veterinary guidance for exact dosing and condition. Ensure the full dose is consumed, potentially by hiding it in food, and consult your vet for long-term use or if any side effects occur.

Precautions

Use with caution in animals with existing liver disease. Not recommended for use in pregnant or nursing animals without specific veterinary approval. Regular monitoring of liver function (e.g., blood work) is recommended, especially during long-term use, to ensure the liver levels are improving.

Contraindications

Do not use in animals with a known hypersensitivity to the ingredients: S-Adenosyl Methionine and Silybin Phospholipid Complex.

Possible side effect

Side effects are rare, but may include: Mild nausea; Stomach upset/vomiting; Diarrhea or constipation; Lethargy. If adverse reactions occur or the pet's condition worsens, discontinue use and contact a veterinarian immediately.

Drug interaction

Ensure your vet is aware of all other medications or supplements your pet is taking to avoid potential interactions.

Missed dose

Give the medicine as soon as you can, but skip the missed dose if it is almost time for your next dose. Do not give two doses at one time.

Overdose

Seek your veterinarian for emergency medical attention.

Storage

Store at room temperature away from moisture and heat. Keep out of reach of the children and pets.
