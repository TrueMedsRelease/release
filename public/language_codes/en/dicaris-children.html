Common use

Dicaris Children is used to treat worm infections, including those caused by pork and dog tapeworms, especially in children. It helps eliminate intestinal worms, preventing further harm and improving digestive health.

Dosage and direction

Take the tablet with a glass of water; do not chew or crush the tablet. It can be taken with or without food.

Precautions

Dicaris Children should be used with caution in patients with liver or kidney disease, as dose adjustments may be necessary. Patients with certain blood disorders should also exercise caution while using Levamisole.

Contraindications

Patients with a history of agranulocytosis, leukopenia, or thrombocytopenia should not use Dicaris Children. Dicaris Children is also contraindicated in patients with severe liver or kidney disease.
In case of any known allergies to levamisole or any antihelminthics, avoid using this tablet. Also, in case your child shows any symptoms of an allergic reaction, take them to the doctor immediately.

Possible side effect

The common side effects of Dicaris Children are: Gastrointestinal distress; Dizziness or lightheadedness; Fatigue; Headache; Flu-Like Symptoms.

Drug interaction

Dicaris Children can interact with anticoagulants (such as warfarin, and heparin), and immunosuppressive medications (such as cyclosporine, and tacrolimus).

Missed dose

Since this medication is mostly a one-time medication, there should be no issues with a missed dose. Consult with a doctor and schedule the dose for another day.

Overdose

If you accidentally take more Dicaris Children than prescribed, you may experience overdose effects like nausea and vomiting. In such a situation, consult your doctor for advice.

Storage

Store Dicaris Children at room temperature and away from direct sunlight. Do not keep this medication in locations of high moisture, like the bathroom. Keep away from children and pets.
