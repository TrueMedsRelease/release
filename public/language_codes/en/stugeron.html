Common use

Stugeron is used to relieve balance disorders and prevent motion sickness by reducing dizziness, nausea, vomiting, and spinning sensations. It is commonly prescribed for vertigo and balance issues related to the inner ear. This medication helps improve stability and alleviate symptoms associated with these conditions.

Dosage and direction

Take Stugeron exactly as prescribed by your doctor. Take the tablet with a glass of water. Swallow the tablet as a whole, do not crush or chew it. Take it in the dosage suggested by your doctor. It can be taken with or without food.

Precautions

Caution is advised in older adults and patients with liver and kidney impairment. This medicine may cause drowsiness. Do not drive or use machines after taking this medicine, as these activities require focus and attention.

Contraindications

Stugeron is contraindicated in patients allergic to cinnarizine, having porphyria, or Parkinson's disease. This medicine is not recommended during pregnancy and breastfeeding women.

Possible side effect

Stugeron may cause side effects such as daytime sleepiness, nausea or vomiting, weight gain, and indigestion. If these side effects persist or interfere with your daily activities, speak with your doctor.

Drug interaction

Avoid taking antidepressants (amitryptiline) or other sedatives with this medicine.

Missed dose

If you forget to take a dose, take it as soon as you remember, unless it's close to the time for your next dose. In this case, skip the missed dose and take your next dose at the usual time.

Overdose

If you or someone else takes an excessive amount of this medication by accident, it is essential to get immediate medical attention from your doctor or nearby hospital.

Storage

Store at room temperature between 20-25°C (68-77°F). Keep the tablets in their original packaging to protect from moisture. Store away from direct sunlight and heat sources. Keep out of reach of children and pets.
