Common use

Theofer XT is a nutritional supplement primarily used for the treatment and prevention of iron deficiency anemia and folate deficiency. It is a hematinic formulation designed to improve iron levels, hemoglobin production, and oxygen transport in the body.

Dosage and direction

It is generally advised to be swallowed whole with water, typically on an empty stomach to maximize iron absorption, though taking it with food may help reduce potential gastrointestinal discomfort. The tablet should be swallowed whole; breaking, chewing, or crushing it is discouraged.

Precautions

Inform your doctor if you have iron overload conditions (hemochromatosis, hemosiderosis), hemolytic anemia, stomach ulcers, inflammatory bowel disease (ulcerative colitis), or vitamin B12 deficiency. It is generally safe for pregnant and lactating women when taken under medical supervision.

Contraindications

Do not take Therofer XT if you are allergic to iron, folic acid, or any other ingredients in the tablet. If you have any planned surgery, you may be asked to stop taking this supplement 2-3 weeks in advance.

Possible side effect

Common side effects may include nausea, constipation, and dark-colored stools.

Drug interaction

Tell your doctor and pharmacist about all of your drugs (prescription or OTC, natural products, vitamins).

Missed dose

Taking the missed dose promptly, or skipping it if the next scheduled time is near. Do not exceed the recommended daily dose.

Overdose

If an overdose is suspected, contact a doctor immediately or go to the nearest hospital emergency department.

Storage

Theofer XT should be stored in a cool, dry place, typically below 25°C to 30°C. Protect the tablets from direct sunlight, heat, and moisture. Keep away from children and pets.
