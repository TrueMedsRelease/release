Common use

Temovate Cream is commonly used to manage and reduce the symptoms of various skin conditions, such as eczema and psoriasis. It belongs to a group of medicines known as corticosteroids. In addition to managing these primary conditions, it can also be effective in dealing with issues like skin inflammation, itching, and redness caused by allergies or other skin disorders. Its soothing properties help reduce discomfort and improve the overall health of your skin.

Dosage and direction

Before application, ensure that the affected area is clean and dry. Use your fingertip or a clean cotton swab to apply the cream to the affected area. The quantity needed will depend on the size of the affected area. Gently massage the cream on the skin until it is fully absorbed. Avoid covering or bandaging unless advised by your doctor. Wash your hands thoroughly after applying the cream to prevent potential spread of infection.

Precautions

Before starting on this medicine, it's vital that you consult your doctor for the right dosage and frequency. Inform your doctor about any pre-existing conditions or ongoing medication before using this cream. If you notice any side effects while using this medicine, notify your doctor immediately. Remember to continue using the cream for the duration prescribed by your doctor for optimal results.

Contraindications

Caution is advised while using Temovate Cream if you are pregnant or breastfeeding. It is best to consult your doctor before applying this medicine.
If you have allergies, particularly to clobetasol propionate or other ingredients in Temovate Cream, do not use this medicine without speaking to your doctor.
Psoriasis: Overuse of Temovate Cream in psoriasis patients can cause rebound flares.
Kidney Disease: Individuals suffering from kidney disease should be cautious when using Temovate Cream due to potential systemic absorption and related complications.

Possible side effect

While using Temovate Cream, you might experience some of the following side effects: Burning or stinging at the application site; Acne; Change in skin color; Stretch marks; Itching.

Drug interaction

Steroid-based Medicines: Topical corticosteroids like hydrocortisone or betamethasone may interact with Temovate Cream, potentially intensifying side effects if used simultaneously.
Immunosuppressants: Medicines like cyclosporine or methotrexate may interact with Temovate Cream, possibly leading to altered medication effects or increased risk of side effects.

Missed dose

Should you forget to apply Temovate Cream at the scheduled time, do so as soon as you remember. However, if it's close to the time for your next application, leave out the forgotten dose and continue with your regular schedule. Never use extra cream to make up for a missed dose without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

If you accidentally apply more Temovate Cream than recommended, you might experience some skin irritation or rash. If this happens, do not panic; simply wash off the excess cream with water. However, if symptoms persist or worsen, inform your doctor.
