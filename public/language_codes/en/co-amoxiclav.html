Common use

Co-Amoxiclav is used to treat bacterial infections such as sinusitis, respiratory tract infections, urinary tract infections, skin and soft tissue infections, and bone and joint infections. Co-Amoxiclav is also used to treat skin, soft tissues, bone and joint infections and dental cavities. Co-Amoxiclav tablet also prevents bacterial growth and aids in symptom and pain relief. This medicine is not effective against viral infections like colds and flu.

Dosage and direction

Co-Amoxiclav should be taken as prescribed by your healthcare provider. Avoid taking tablet on an empty stomach. Do not crush or chew tablet. Consume the tablet with a glass of water.

Precautions

Co-Amoxiclav can interact with diseases, including: Liver disease; Glandular fever; Colitis. Consult with your doctor.

Contraindications

Co-Amoxiclav is contraindicated in patients with a history of hypersensitivity to Amoxicillin, Clavulanic Acid or other cephalosporin antibiotics. It is also contraindicated in patients with history of jaundice or hepatic dysfunction.

Possible side effect

Below are some common side effects of Co-Amoxiclav: Vomiting: Initial side effect is vomiting as the components may induce it; Nausea: Patients may feel nauseous while taking the Co-Amoxiclav tablet; Diarrhea: Vomiting may be accompanied by diarrhea after consuming the tablet; Mucocutaneous candidiasis: The components of Co-Amoxiclav may cause candidiasis bacteria to multiply.

Drug interaction

Co-Amoxiclav can interact with medications, including: Allopurinol; Probenecid; Blood thinners (warfarin); Anticancer, or anti-arthritis drug (methotrexate); Estrogen / Progesterone contraceptive.

Missed dose

If you forgot to take Co-Amoxiclav tablet, take it as soon as you remember. If the time for the next dosage is close, skipping the missed one is recommended now and resume your regular dosage pattern. Never increase your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

If you or someone else takes an excessive amount of Co-Amoxiclav by accident, it is essential to take immediate medical attention from your doctor or nearby hospital. Symptoms of an overdose include vomiting, nausea, and diarrhea.
