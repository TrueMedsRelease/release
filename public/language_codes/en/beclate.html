Common use

Beclate inhaler is a steroid which is used to prevent and treat asthma. It inhibits inflammation in the lungs and airway tract when inhaled, expanding the airway and making breathing easier.

Dosage and direction

Follow your doctor's instructions and read the label before using Beclate inhaler. The inhaler should be shaken. Press down on the inhaler one time to release the drug while inhaling in through your mouth, then hold your breath for 10 seconds. repeat till you've taken the recommended number of puffs from the doctor. After that, spit out the water and rinse your mouth completely.

Precautions

The use of Beclate during pregnancy and breastfeeding should be discussed with your doctor, as it should only be used if the potential benefits outweigh the risks.
Patients with a history of tuberculosis or ocular herpes simplex should use Beclate with caution as it may worsen these conditions.

Contraindications

Beclate is contraindicated in patients with known allergies to Beclomethasone and any of its components.
It is also contraindicated in patients with active or untreated fungal, bacterial, or viral infections.

Possible side effect

The majority of side effects are minor and will go away as your body adjusts to the medicine. If they don't go away or you're concerned about them, see your doctor. Side effects of Beclate: Throat irritation; Sore throat; Sore tongue; Cough; Headache.

Drug interaction

Beclate (Beclomethasone) may interact with antifungal medication (eg., ketoconazole), HIV protease inhibitors (eg., ritonavir), and antibiotics (eg., clarithromycin).

Missed dose

If you forget to administer this medicine, do it as soon as you remember. But avoid repeating the dose.

Overdose

Overdose of this medication may cause symptoms like nausea, vomiting, muscle weakness, depression, and loss of appetite. If you observe any of these symptoms, inform your doctor immediately.

Storage

Store Beclate at room temperature, away from heat, moisture, and sunlight. Keep the inhaler tightly closed and out of reach of children and pets.
