Common use

Advent DT (Dispersible Tablet) is used to treat bacterial infections affecting the respiratory tract, urinary tract, skin, ear, sinus, and soft tissues. Amoxicillin is a penicillin antibiotic that kills bacteria, while Clavulanic Acid prevents bacteria from becoming resistant. This combination is effective against bronchitis, pneumonia, sinusitis, tonsillitis, urinary tract infections (UTIs), and skin infections.

Dosage and direction

Follow the dosage and timing instructions provided by your doctor precisely. Do not change or exceed the prescribed dose without consulting your doctor. Consult your doctor before discontinuing the medication; do not stop taking it without their guidance.

Precautions

Caution is advised while taking this medicine during pregnancy and breastfeeding. Caution is advised in patients with kidney and liver impairment.
In patients who have diarrhea because of Clostridium difficile bacteria, this medication could make the condition worse.

Contraindications

Advent DT is contraindicated in patients with a history of hypersensitivity to Amoxicillin, Clavulanic Acid or other cephalosporin antibiotics. It is also contraindicated in patients with history of jaundice or hepatic dysfunction.

Possible side effect

The following are some of the possible side effects you might notice after taking Advent DT: Diarrhea; Nausea; Vomiting; Skin rashes; Abdominal discomfort; Flatulence.

Drug interaction

Avoid taking anticoagulants (warfarin), antigout medications (probenecid), oral contraceptives, immunosuppressants (mycophenolate mofetil) and other antibiotics with this medicine. These medications may alter the safety and effectiveness of Amoxicillin and Clavulanic Acid.

Missed dose

If you forget to take a dose of Advent DT, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it is nearly time for your next dose, skip the missed one and stick to your regular schedule. Never increase the dose without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

An overdose of Advent DT may result in side effects like diarrhea and skin rashes. If you suspect an overdose, contact your doctor for guidance.

Storage

Keep Advent DT in a cool, dry place, below 30°C. Avoid exposure to direct sunlight and damp conditions. Keep this medicine out of reach of children and pets.
