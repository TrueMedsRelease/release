Common use

Asthalin Inhaler is used to treat asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). It helps relieve symptoms such as wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath by relaxing the muscles in the airways and making breathing easier.

Dosage and direction

Before using Asthalin Inhaler, shake it well to ensure proper mixing of the medication.
Remove the cap from the mouthpiece and breathe out gently to empty your lungs.
Place the mouthpiece between your lips and close your lips tightly around it. Do not bite or chew on the mouthpiece.
As you begin to inhale slowly through your mouth, press down on the canister to release one puff of medication into your lungs.
Continue inhaling slowly until your lungs are full, then hold your breath for a few seconds before exhaling slowly.
If another dose is required, wait at least one minute before repeating these steps.
Remember to consult your doctor for specific advice on how to use Asthalin Inhaler correctly.

Precautions

If you're pregnant or planning to become pregnant, talk to your doctor before using Asthalin Inhaler. It may not be suitable for use during pregnancy.
Consult your doctor if you're breastfeeding before using Asthalin Inhaler. They will advise you on the potential risks and benefits.
Avoid consuming alcohol while using Asthalin Inhaler, as it may worsen side effects.
Asthalin Inhaler may cause dizziness or shakiness. If affected, avoid operating machinery or driving until these symptoms subside.

Contraindications

It is important to use Asthalin Inhaler with caution if you have any of the following conditions:
Diabetes: Asthalin Inhaler may affect blood sugar levels.
Thyroid problems: The medication may interact with thyroid medications.
High blood pressure: Asthalin Inhaler can increase heart rate and blood pressure.
Heart disease: Consult your doctor before using Asthalin Inhaler if you have a history of heart problems.
Allergic reactions to salbutamol or any other ingredients in the inhaler.

Possible side effect

Taking Asthalin Inhaler may lead to some side effects. Here are a few side effects you might experience: Headaches; Shakiness; Increased heart rate; Shortness of breath; Swelling of the face, lips, or eyelids.

Drug interaction

Certain medicines can interact with Asthalin Inhaler: Beta-blockers: Propranolol, atenolol; Diuretics: Furosemide, hydrochlorothiazide; Monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs): Phenelzine, tranylcypromine; Antidepressants: Amitriptyline, fluoxetine.

Missed dose

If you forget to take a dose of Asthalin Inhaler, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it is almost time for your next dose, skip the missed dose and continue with your regular schedule. Never increase your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

Taking too much Asthalin Inhaler can lead to side effects such as headaches, shakiness, increased heart rate, shortness of breath and swelling of the face, lips, or eyelids. If you accidentally takes too much dose, seek immediate medical attention.

Storage

Store Asthalin Inhaler in a cool and dry place. Protect the inhaler from light and moisture. Keep it out of reach of children and pets. Use the product before its expiry date.
