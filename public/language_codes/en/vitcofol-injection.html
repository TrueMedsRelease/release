Common use

Vitcofol Injection is used to treat anaemia caused by folic acid and vitamin B12 deficiency. It helps improve red blood cell production and boosts energy levels. This medicine is also used to manage nerve-related problems like peripheral neuropathy. It may be prescribed during pregnancy to correct deficiencies and support normal foetal development under medical supervision.

Dosage and direction

The dosage and frequency of the Vitcofol injection are determined by the patient's medical condition, age, and other factors, as well as the healthcare professional's discretion. Before administering the injection, the healthcare professional should ensure that the injection site is clean and sterile. The injection should be given slowly and deeply into the muscle to minimize discomfort and the risk of tissue damage.

Precautions

Vitcofol injection should be used during pregnancy only if recommended by a healthcare provider. The injection may have some risks to the developing foetus, and the potential benefits and risks should be evaluated before use.
Patients with cardiovascular disease may be at increased risk of adverse effects from Vitcofol injection, including thrombosis or heart attack. Vitcofol injection should be used with caution in patients with cardiovascular disease.
Vitcofol injection contains certain vitamins that may be metabolized in the liver. Patients with liver disease may be at increased risk of adverse effects from Vitcofol injection. Vitcofol injection should be used with caution in patients with liver disease.
Vitcofol injection may lower the seizure threshold in patients with seizure disorders. It is advisable to exercise caution when administering Vitcofol injection to these patients and ensure that a healthcare provider closely supervises its use.

Contraindications

Inform your healthcare provider if you are allergic to Vitcofol injection or any of its ingredients. Allergic reactions may occur, ranging from a mild rash or itching to severe anaphylactic reactions.

Possible side effect

Vitcofol Injection may cause side effects such as nausea, vomiting, stomach pain, flushing, and pain or swelling at the injection site. These effects are generally mild and temporary. Persistent or severe symptoms should be brought to the attention of your doctor. Proper administration by a trained healthcare professional can minimize injection site discomfort.

Missed dose

Healthcare professionals normally administer Vitcofol injection in a hospital or clinic setting, minimizing the risk of a missed dose. However, if you suspect a dose was not given as scheduled, you must notify your doctor or nurse.

Overdose

A healthcare professional, such as a doctor or nurse, administers Vitcofol injection, ensuring that the treatment and dosage are tailored to meet the individual needs of the patient, thereby reducing the risk of overdose. However, if the patient experiences any discomfort or adverse effects during or after the administration of the injection, it is essential to inform the healthcare provider immediately.
