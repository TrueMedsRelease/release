Common use

Tenovate Cream is commonly used to manage and reduce the symptoms of various skin conditions, such as eczema and psoriasis. It belongs to a group of medicines known as corticosteroids.
In addition to managing these primary conditions, it can also be effective in dealing with issues like skin inflammation, itching, and redness caused by allergies or other skin disorders. Its soothing properties help reduce discomfort and improve the overall health of your skin.

Dosage and direction

To use Tenovate Cream properly, follow these guidelines:
Before application, ensure that the affected area is clean and dry.
Use your fingertip or a clean cotton swab to apply the cream to the affected area.
The quantity needed will depend on the size of the affected area.
Gently massage the cream on the skin until it is fully absorbed. Avoid covering or bandaging unless advised by your doctor.
Wash your hands thoroughly after applying the cream to prevent potential spread of infection.

Precautions

Contact you doctor if you have any of these conditions: High blood sugar (diabetes); Large areas of burned or damaged skin; Liver disease; Skin infection; An unusual or allergic reaction to clobetasol, steroids, other medications, foods, dyes, or preservatives; Pregnant or trying to get pregnant; Breast-feeding.

Contraindications

Psoriasis: Overuse of Tenovate Cream in psoriasis patients can cause rebound flares.
Kidney Disease: Individuals suffering from kidney disease should be cautious when using Tenovate Cream due to potential systemic absorption and related complications.
Allergic: An unusual or allergic reaction to clobetasol, steroids, other medications, foods, dyes, or preservatives.

Possible side effect

While using Tenovate Cream, you might experience some of the following side effects: Burning or stinging at the application site; Acne; Change in skin colour; Stretch marks; Itching.
Most side effects are temporary and generally harmless, which resolve on discontinuing the use of Tenovate Cream. However, if you experience any serious side effects or worsening of any of the symptoms, please consult your doctor.

Drug interaction

Tenovate Cream interacts with the following medicines:
Steroid-based Medicines: Topical corticosteroids like hydrocortisone or betamethasone may interact with Tenovate Cream, potentially intensifying side effects if used simultaneously.
Immunosuppressants: Medicines like cyclosporine or methotrexate may interact with Tenovate Cream, possibly leading to altered medication effects or increased risk of side effects.

Missed dose

Should you forget to apply Tenovate Cream at the scheduled time, do so as soon as you remember. However, if it's close to the time for your next application, leave out the forgotten dose and continue with your regular schedule. Never use extra cream to make up for a missed dose without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

If you accidentally apply more Tenovate Cream than recommended, you might experience some skin irritation or rash. If this happens, do not panic; simply wash off the excess cream with water. However, if symptoms persist or worsen, inform your doctor.
