Common use

Cepmox capsule is primarily used to manage a variety of bacterial infections. It belongs to the antibiotic class of medicines. In addition to tackling a multitude of bacterial infections, this medicine is also effective against some sexually transmitted diseases, like gonorrhoea. Moreover, this medicine is sometimes prescribed to lessen the risk of duodenal ulcers linked to Helicobacter pylori bacteria.

Dosage and direction

Swallow the Cepmox capsule whole with water. This medicine can be taken with or without food. Try to take your doses at evenly spaced intervals at the same times each day. Continue taking the medicine for the full duration prescribed by your doctor, even if you begin to feel better.

Precautions

Liver disease: Caution is advised if you have liver problems, as your body might process this medicine slower, leading to potential side effects.
Kidney disease: Dialysis patients or those with severe renal impairment may need a lower dosage, as high doses could cause kidney damage.
Mononucleosis (glandular fever): If you have glandular fever, use this medicine cautiously, as it might increase the risk of a skin rash.

Contraindications

Cepmox is contraindicated for individuals who have a known allergy to penicillin or other beta-lactam antibiotics.
Patients with mononucleosis (an infection caused by the Epstein-Barr virus) are advised not to take Cepmox, due to an increased risk of skin rashes.

Possible side effect

Taking Cepmox capsule might lead to the following side effects: Nausea; Vomiting; Diarrhea; Skin rash; Itching.

Drug interaction

Oral contraceptives: Cepmox may lessen the effectiveness of birth control pills like estradiol and ethinyl estradiol.
Anticoagulants: When taken with blood thinners such as warfarin, Cepmox capsule can heighten the risk of bleeding.
Antigout medications: Combining Cepmox with gout medications, such as allopurinol and probenecid, can increase the incidence of side effects like skin rashes.
Other antibiotics: Cepmox capsule could interact with other antibiotics like tetracycline or doxycycline, potentially reducing their efficacy.

Missed dose

If you forget to take a dose of Cepmox, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it's almost time for your next dose, skip the missed one and continue with your regular schedule. Never increase your dosage without asking your doctor first.

Overdose

Overdosing on Cepmox may cause symptoms like severe nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal pain. If you experience these signs after taking more than your prescribed dose, inform your doctor.

Storage

Store Cepmox capsule at room temperature in a cool, dry area. Keep away from direct sunlight. Keep the medicine in its original packaging to protect it from moisture. Ensure the medicine is out of reach of children. Use the medicine before its expiration date.
