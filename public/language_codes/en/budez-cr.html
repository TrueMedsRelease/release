Common use

Budez CR capsule is primarily used to manage moderate to severe ulcerative colitis, a type of inflammatory bowel disease. This medicine falls under the category of corticosteroids. In addition to its main use, this medicine can be used to alleviate the symptoms of other types of inflammatory bowel diseases such as Crohn's disease and microscopic colitis. It works by reducing inflammation in the digestive tract, thereby helping to relieve symptoms like abdominal pain and frequent diarrhea.

Dosage and direction

Follow the dosage and timing instructions provided by your doctor precisely. Do not change or exceed the prescribed dose without consulting your doctor. Consult your doctor before discontinuing the medication; do not stop taking it without their guidance. Adhere to the dietary recommendations given by your doctor.

Precautions

During pregnancy, it is advised to only take Budez CR capsule if the benefits outweigh potential risks. Please consult your doctor before starting this medicine if you are pregnant or planning to conceive.
If you are breastfeeding, it's crucial to consult with your doctor about taking Budez CR. The active ingredient, budesonide, may pass into breast milk.
Caution is suggested when taking Budez CR capsule if you have any liver health issues. Consulting your doctor is recommended before starting this medicine as it may affect liver function.
If you have a history of kidney problems, be cautious when using Budez CR capsule. It would be best to discuss any kidney-related concerns with your doctor before using this medicine.

Contraindications

Before taking Budez CR, inform your doctor if you have any allergies. This includes known allergies to budesonide or any other ingredients in the capsule.
Diabetes: Budez CR capsule can affect blood sugar control, making it crucial to monitor glucose levels closely.
Hypertension: This medication might alter blood pressure control, so regular monitoring is essential if you have high blood pressure.
Osteoporosis: Long-term use of Budez CR could exacerbate bone loss in patients already suffering from osteoporosis.

Possible side effect

Using Budez CR capsule may cause the following common side effects: Headache; Nausea; Abdominal pain; Bloating; Gas; Acne; Mood changes.

Drug interaction

Antifungal Medicines: Medications like ketoconazole and other antifungals can increase the level of budesonide in your body.
Antibiotics: Certain antibiotics like erythromycin and clarithromycin may also increase budesonide levels.
Oral Contraceptives: Oestrogen-containing contraceptives can elevate budesonide levels.
Tuberculosis Medication: Rifampin may reduce the effect of Budez CR Capsule by decreasing budesonide levels.

Missed dose

If you've forgotten to take a dose of Budez CR, take it as soon as you remember. But if it's nearly time for your next dose, just skip this missed one and continue with your regular schedule. Never increase your dosage without consulting your doctor, even if you've missed a dose.

Overdose

Accidentally taking more than the prescribed dose of Budez CR might cause you to experience side effects like muscle weakness and headaches. Your blood sugar levels might also rise. If these symptoms persist, it's important to inform your doctor.
