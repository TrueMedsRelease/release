Common use

Biotin VB7 tablets are primarily used to treat and prevent Vitamin B7 deficiency, promoting healthier hair, skin, and nails by boosting keratin production. They are commonly prescribed to reduce hair fall, strengthen brittle nails, and improve skin texture. These tablets also assist in metabolic processes, converting food into energy.

Dosage and direction

Typically, one tablet is taken daily after a meal, but it should be used as prescribed by a doctor. Swallow the tablet whole; do not crush, chew, or break it. Taking the supplement at the same time each day may help maintain consistent levels, though individual needs vary.

Precautions

Consult a doctor before use if you have kidney disease, are undergoing dialysis, or have bleeding disorders.
High-dose biotin can cause false readings in tests for thyroid, heart health (troponin), and hormone levels. Inform doctors 2-3 weeks before tests.
Pregnancy or breastfeeding: Only use if necessary and advised by a healthcare professional.

Possible side effect

Biotin VB7 tablets are generally safe, but may cause mild side effects like stomach upset, nausea, diarrhea, or cramping. Rare, more severe reactions include skin rashes or allergic reactions.

Drug interaction

Other drugs may affect Biotin VB7, including prescription and over-the-counter medicines, vitamins, and herbal products. Tell your doctor about all other medicines you use.

Missed dose

Take the medicine as soon as you can, but skip the missed dose if it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take two doses at one time.

Overdose

Seek emergency medical attention.

Storage

Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight, moisture, and out of the reach of children and pets.
