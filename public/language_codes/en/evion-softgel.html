Common use

Evion Softgel Vitamin E (Alpha Tocopheryl Acetate), a potent antioxidant used primarily to treat vitamin E deficiency, protect cells from oxidative stress, and support skin, hair, and immune health. It is commonly used to improve skin moisture, stimulate hair growth, treat acne, and reduce muscle cramps.

Dosage and direction

Take one capsule daily or as directed by a physician.

Precautions

Caution is advised during pregnancy and breastfeeding. Caution is advised in patients with bleeding conditions due to Vitamin K deficiency, a history of thrombosis (a clot inside a blood vessel), a history of prostate cancer and impaired kidney function.

Contraindications

Evion Softgel is contraindicated in patients with a history of hypersensitivity to Vitamin E.

Possible side effect

Side effects of Evion Softgel: Diarrhea, Stomach upset.

Drug interaction

Avoid taking anticoagulants (warfarin), antiplatelet agents (acetylsalicylic acid, clopidogrel, and ticlopidine), thrombolytics, HIV medications (tipranavir), oestrogens, lipase inhibitor (orlistat), cholesterol-lowering agents (colestyramine and colestipol) and iron-containing medicines, anticonvulsants (phenobarbital, phenytoin and carbamazepine). These medications may alter the safety and effectiveness of Vitamin E.

Missed dose

If you miss a dose of Evion Softgel, take it as soon as you remember. But, if the next dose is near, skip the missed dose and resume the regular dosing regime.

Overdose

Overdose can cause nausea, diarrhea, flatulence, tiredness, lack of energy, headache, blurred vision, and dermatitis. If an overdose is suspected, Evion Softgel supplementation should be stopped. Strict adherence to the prescribed dose and duration is essential to avoid overdosage.

Storage

Keep away from direct sunlight, heat and moisture. Keep out of the reach of children and pets.
