Common use

Temsujohn tablet is primarily used to manage the symptoms of a common condition in men known as benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH). This medicine falls under a category of medications known as alpha-blockers. BPH causes the prostate gland to enlarge, resulting in urination problems. In addition to its primary use, this medicine also helps decrease the frequent or urgent need to urinate, including during the night. It assists in improving the initiation of urine flow and enhancing the strength of the urine stream.

Dosage and direction

Swallow the tablet whole with a glass of water. Do not chew, crush or open the tablet. It's best to take this medicine 30 minutes after the same meal each day. Do not increase the dose on your own; please consult your doctor.

Precautions

You're advised to limit alcohol intake while taking Temsujohn as it might increase the risk of dizziness and lightheadedness.
For individuals with liver health issues, it's recommended to discuss with your doctor before starting Temsujohn.
If you have any kidney problems, consultation with your doctor is advisable prior to using the Temsujohn tablet.
Temsujohn tablet might cause dizziness or drowsiness. Until you know how this medication affects you, avoid driving or operating machinery.
Older patients might be more susceptible to side effects of Temsujohn tablet and should take this medicine under medical supervision.

Contraindications

Low blood pressure: As Temsujohn lowers blood pressure, it should be used with caution in patients who already have low blood pressure.
Prostate Cancer: Temsujohn tablet is not indicated for use in patients with prostate cancer, as it is primarily used to treat benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH).
Before starting Temsujohn, inform your doctor if you have any allergies, especially to Tamsulosin or other alpha-blockers.
Temsujohn is not recommended for use in children below 18 years of age. Always consult your doctor before giving any medicine to a child.

Possible side effect

Temsujohn may have the following side effects: Dizziness; Abnormal ejaculation: This can include a decrease in the volume of ejaculation; Runny or stuffy nose; Headache; Weakness; Back pain; Diarrhoea.
Most common side effects are temporary and generally harmless, which resolve upon discontinuing the use of Temsujohn. However, if you experience any serious side effects or worsening of any of the symptoms, please consult your doctor.

Drug interaction

Temsujohn tablet interacts with the following medicines:
Alpha-blockers: The interaction with prazosin or terazosin can cause further lowering of blood pressure.
Erectile dysfunction medications: Medicines like sildenafil can intensify the blood pressure-lowering effects of Temsujohn tablet.
Antifungals: Interaction with itraconazole and ketoconazole may increase the concentration of Temsujohn tablet in your body, leading to increased side effects.
HIV protease inhibitors: Medicines like lopinavir and ritonavir can increase the blood levels of Temsujohn, increasing the risk of side effects.
Antibiotics: Clarithromycin can slow down the removal of Temsujohn from your body, resulting in increased side effects.

Missed dose

If you happen to forget a dose of Temsujohn, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it's nearly time for your next dose, skip the missed one and carry on with your regular schedule. Never adjust your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

In case you've taken more than the prescribed amount of Temsujohn, you may experience symptoms like severe dizziness or fainting. If you notice such symptoms, it's advisable to call your doctor.
