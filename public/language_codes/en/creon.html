Common use

Creon is a medication used to treat exocrine pancreatic insufficiency (EPI), a condition in which the pancreas does not produce enough digestive enzymes. Creon is classified as a pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy (PERT) and contains three essential enzymes, lipase, protease, and amylase. These enzymes replace those that the body cannot produce in adequate amounts and are necessary for breaking down fats, proteins, and carbohydrates during digestion.

Dosage and direction

Creon is an oral capsule taken with meals and snacks, usually three meals a day and two or three snacks. Capsules should be swallowed whole, or for patients who are unable to swallow capsule contents, may be sprinkled onto a small amount of soft food and taken as directed, without chewing.

Precautions

To make sure Creon is safe for you, tell your doctor if you have ever had: kidney disease; gout; high blood uric acid levels (called hyperuricemia); diabetes; a blockage or scarring in your intestines; trouble swallowing pills; lactose intolerance; or or pork protein allergy.
Tell your doctor if you are pregnant or plan to get pregnant, or if you are breastfeeding or plan to breastfeed.

Contraindications

Call your doctor at once if you have symptoms of a rare but serious bowel disorder: severe or unusual stomach pain, vomiting, bloating, diarrhea, or constipation.
Do not chew or keep Creon in your mouth. Creon can irritate the inside of your mouth.

Possible side effect

Common Creon side effects may include: stomach pain, gas, vomiting; diarrhea, frequent or abnormal bowel movements; rectal itching; headache, dizziness; runny or stuffy nose, cough, sore throat; or changes in your blood sugar.

Drug interaction

Other drugs may interact with Creon, including prescription and over-the-counter medicines, vitamins, and herbal products. Tell your doctor about all your current medicines and any medicine you start or stop using.

Missed dose

Take the missed dose with your next meal or snack. Do not take extra medicine to make up the missed dose.

Overdose

Seek emergency medical attention.

Storage

Store at room temperature between 59°F to 77°F (15°C to 25°C). Avoid heat. Keep Creon in a dry place and in the original container. Keep away from children and pets.
