Common use

Zincoheal is used for managing mild nutritional deficiencies and maintaining overall balance in dietary intake. This dietary supplement contains a blend of vitamins and minerals, such as Zinc and Vitamin C, which are essential for supporting immune system functionality and promoting skin and hair health.

Dosage and direction

Take one tablet once a day or as directed by your healthcare professional.

Precautions

Zincoheal supplement may be used during pregnancy and breastfeeding under medical supervision, as specific vitamins and minerals support fetal development and maternal health.
Exercise caution in patients with kidney or liver disorders, thyroid conditions, or those undergoing treatment for cancer or cardiovascular disease. Monitoring of vitamin and mineral levels may be necessary in such individuals.

Contraindications

Avoid Zincoheal supplement if you are allergic to any of its ingredients, including any vitamin, mineral, or excipient. Patients with hypervitaminosis A or D should not use this formulation.

Possible side effect

Side effects of Zincoheal: Nausea; Upset stomach; Metallic taste; Headache; Mild diarrhea.

Drug interaction

Avoid taking other supplements containing the same vitamins or minerals to prevent overdose. Caution is advised when used with tetracycline antibiotics, quinolone antibiotics, thyroid medications (levothyroxine), diuretics (furosemide), and antacids, as these may interfere with absorption or effectiveness.

Missed dose

If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember. If it's almost time for your next dose, skip the missed dose and continue with your regular schedule. Avoid double-dosing.

Overdose

An overdose may lead to toxicity symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and dizziness, especially with fat-soluble vitamins like A, D, and E. If an overdose is suspected, contact emergency services immediately.

Storage

Store in a cool, dry and dark place. Keep away from direct sunlight, heat and moisture. Keep out of the reach of children and pets.
