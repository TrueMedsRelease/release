Common use

Duratia (dapoxetine) is a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) specifically designed to treat premature ejaculation in adult men aged 18 to 64 years. It works by raising serotonin levels in the nervous system, which helps delay ejaculation and improve control over it's timing. This enhances overall sexual satisfaction and increases the time it takes to ejaculate.

Dosage and direction

To ensure the best results, taking the Duratia is recommended according to the dosage and duration prescribed by your doctor. The tablet should be swallowed whole, not chewed, crushed, or broken. You can take the Duratia tablet with or without food, but it is advisable to take it at a fixed daily time. Typically, it is taken 1 to 3 hours before sexual activity is expected and should not be taken more than once within 24 hours.

Precautions

Duratia tablet can interact with conditions/diseases, including: Cardiovascular/heart disease; A history of dizziness due to low blood pressure; Depression; Mania; Bipolar disorder; Schizophrenia; Epilepsy/fits; Bleeding or blood clotting problems; Glaucoma; Kidney problems; Moderate to severe liver problems. It is advisable to consult your doctor before using the medication.

Contraindications

It is contraindicated in patients with a history of hypersensitivity to Dapoxetine, patients having heart conditions, bleeding or clotting disorder, epilepsy, mental health problems like bipolar disorder, depression, or mania; glaucoma, liver diseases, and tendency to faint.
The Duratia tablet is not intended for use in women and children.

Possible side effect

Side effects of Duratia: Feeling dizzy; Lightheaded; Feeling sick; Feeling sweaty; Feeling confused; Headache.

Drug interaction

Avoid taking antipsychotic medications (benzodiazepines), anticoagulant medications (acenocoumarol), cancer medications (abemaciclib), antidiabetic medications (acarbose), antihypertensives (beta-blockers such as acebutolol), and non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (aceclofenac). These medications may alter the safety and effectiveness of Duratia.

Missed dose

If you miss a dose of the Duratia, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it is almost time for your next scheduled dose, skip the missed dose and continue with your regular dosing schedule. Avoid taking a double dose to make up for the missed one.

Overdose

Exceeding the recommended dose of Duratia may lead to dizziness, headache, nausea, dry mouth, and indigestion. If you suspect an overdose, it is crucial to seek immediate medical attention.
