Common use

Ivermectol tablet is a medication used to manage worm infections in the intestines. It is commonly prescribed by doctors to treat various types of worm infections. This medicine is effective against roundworms, threadworms, and hookworms, among others.

Dosage and direction

Take Ivermectol tablet exactly as prescribed by your doctor. Swallow the tablet with a glass of water. Take it before meals as directed by your doctor.

Precautions

While using Ivermectol tablet, there are some precautions you need to keep in mind. If you are allergic to ivermectin or have heart, liver, or kidney diseases, it is advisable not to take this medicine. Additionally, pregnant women should avoid using this medicine due to the limited safety information available.

Contraindications

If you have a history of heart, liver, or kidney diseases, it's important to consult your doctor before taking Ivermectol. Patients with these conditions may require special monitoring or adjustments in the dosage of the medicine. It's also crucial to mention any other medical conditions you have with your doctor before starting this medication.

Possible side effect

Taking Ivermectol may cause some side effects. Here are a few common ones to be aware of:
Dizziness: You may feel lightheaded or unsteady.
Nausea and vomiting: You may experience an upset stomach and vomiting.
Stomach discomfort: You may feel pain or discomfort in your abdominal area.
Diarrhoea or constipation: Your bowel movements may become loose or infrequent.
Tiredness: You might feel more fatigued than usual.

Drug interaction

Here are some categories of medications and specific examples that may interact with Ivermectol tablet:
Anticoagulants: Warfarin, Heparin.
Antiepileptic medications: Carbamazepine, Phenytoin.
Immunosuppressants: Cyclosporine, Tacrolimus.
Benzodiazepines: Diazepam, Alprazolam.
Antifungal medications: Ketoconazole, Fluconazole.

Missed dose

If you forget to take Ivermectol as instructed, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it is close to the time for your next scheduled dose, skip the missed one and continue with your regular dosage schedule. Never increase your dosage without consulting your doctor.

Overdose

If you accidentally take too much of Ivermectol, seek immediate medical attention. Taking an overdose can lead to side effects such as headache, dizziness, nausea, vomiting, stomach pain, diarrhoea, weakness, skin rash, swelling, shortness of breath, or fits. Contact your doctor or visit a nearby hospital.

Storage

Store Ivermectol in a cool and dry place. Protect the medicine from direct sunlight, moisture, and heat. Keep the medicine out of reach of children to prevent accidental ingestion. Use the product before its expiry date to ensure its effectiveness.
