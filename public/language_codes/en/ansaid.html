Common use

Ansaid (Flurbiprofen) is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to relieve symptoms of arthritis (osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis), such as inflammation, swelling, stiffness, and joint pain.

Dosage and direction

For safe and effective use of this medicine, do not use more of it, do not use it more often, and do not use it for a longer time than ordered by your doctor. Swallow the tablet whole. Do not cut, break, or chew it. When used for severe or continuing arthritis, this medicine must be taken regularly as ordered by your doctor in order for it to help you.

Precautions

Tell your healthcare provider about all of your health conditions and any prescription or over-the-counter (OTC) medicines, vitamins/minerals, herbal products, and other supplements you are using. This will help them determine if flurbiprofen is right for you. Tell your healthcare provider if you have any of the following: Liver or kidney problems; High blood pressure; Asthma; Heart conditions including heart failure or a recent heart attack; Anemia; High potassium levels.

Contraindications

People who are allergic to any of the following should not take Ansaid: Flurbiprofen; Aspirin or any other medicine known as a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory (NSAID).
Do not take Ansaid right before or after a heart surgery called coronary artery bypass graft (CABG). Taking flurbiprofen during this time can increase your risk of heart attack or stroke.
Do not take Ansaid 20 weeks or later in pregnancy without talking to your healthcare provider. Taking flurbiprofen during this time may cause harm to an unborn baby and complications during delivery. Flurbiprofen passes into breast milk. Tell your healthcare provider if you are breastfeeding or plan to breastfeed. Your healthcare provider will advise you if you should take Ansaid while breastfeeding.

Possible side effect

Common side effects of Ansaid include: upset stomach, stomach pain, constipation, diarrhea, gas, bloating, heartburn, nausea, vomiting, dizziness, drowsiness, fatigue, headache, nervousness, skin itching or rash, dry mouth, increased sweating, runny nose, blurred vision, or ringing in your ears.

Drug interaction

Ansaid may interact with blood thinners, diuretics (water pills), cyclosporine, lithium, methotrexate, steroids, aspirin or NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors, or beta-blockers. Tell your doctor all medications you use.

Missed dose

If you miss a dose of this medicine, take it as soon as possible. However, if it is almost time for your next dose, skip the missed dose and go back to your regular dosing schedule. Do not double doses.

Overdose

If you or someone else has used too much Ansaid, get medical help right away.

Storage

Store the medicine in a closed container at room temperature, away from heat, moisture, and direct light. Keep from freezing. Keep out of the reach of children and pets.
