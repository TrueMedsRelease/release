Common use

Latisse Ophthalmic Solution are used to manage open-angle glaucoma and ocular hypertension by reducing increased pressure within the eye. It is a combination medicine that belongs to the class of eye care solutions. In addition to lowering eye pressure, this medicine helps prevent potential damage to the optic nerve and preserves vision in patients with these eye conditions. By improving the drainage of fluid from the eye, it effectively manages the inner eye pressure that could otherwise lead to vision problems if left untreated.

Dosage and direction

Follow these instructions while using Latisse Ophthalmic Solution: Wash your hands thoroughly before using the eye drops. Remove contact lenses before applying the drops. Tilt your head back and pull down your lower eyelid gently. Hold the dropper close to your eye without touching any part of your eye. Squeeze one drop into the pocket formed by your lower eyelid. Close your eyes and press your finger against the inner corner for 1-2 minutes. Replace the cap immediately after use.

Precautions

Caution is advised while using Latisse Ophthalmic Solution if you are pregnant or if you are breastfeeding. It is best to consult your doctor before using this medicine.
Intraocular inflammation: Using these drops during active eye inflammation may worsen the condition or reduce effectiveness.
Eye infections: Application during untreated eye infections could potentially interfere with healing or worsen infection symptoms.

Contraindications

Latisse Ophthalmic Solution is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to bimatoprost or any inactive ingredient in the formulation. It is also contraindicated in patients with active intraocular inflammation or risk factors for macular edema.

Possible side effect

Latisse Ophthalmic Solution may have the following side effects: Eye redness; Itching sensation in the eye; Burning feeling in the eye; Darkening of eyelid skin (The skin around your eyelids may become slightly darker in color with continued use); Increased iris pigmentation (The colored part of your eye (iris) may gradually become darker or change slightly, particularly in people with light-colored eyes).

Drug interaction

Other glaucoma medicines: When used with other eye drops like timolol or latanoprost, wait at least 5 minutes between applications to prevent washing out the previous medicine.
Ophthalmic medications: Simultaneous use with eye medicines like prednisolone or ciprofloxacin may reduce effectiveness if not properly spaced apart during application.

Missed dose

If you forget to instill Latisse Ophthalmic Solution, use them as soon as you remember. If it's nearly time for your next dose, skip the missed one and continue with your regular schedule. Never instill extra drops to make up for a skipped dose.

Overdose

In case you happen to put a few extra drops of this medicine in your eye, do not panic. Rinse your eyes with clean water at room temperature. If any symptoms persist or worsen, inform your doctor.
