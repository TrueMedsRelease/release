Common use

Nutrolin-B Syrup is a, primarily pediatric, probiotic and vitamin supplement used to treat diarrhea, restore healthy intestinal flora, and manage digestive disturbances. It combines Lactic acid bacillus, Nicotinamide (Vitamin B3), and Pyridoxine (Vitamin B6) to improve digestion, support metabolism, and boost immunity.

Dosage and direction

Shake the bottle well to loosen the powder. Add boiled and cooled water up to the mark on the bottle and shake vigorously. Adjust the volume to the mark with more water and shake again. Consume the syrup within 7 days of preparation.
For children above one year: Take 5 ml (1/2 measure) twice daily. For children below one year: Take 2.5 ml (1/4 measure) once daily.

Precautions

This product is for pediatric use. It is not recommended for pregnant or breastfeeding women unless advised by a healthcare professional. Always consult your doctor before taking any supplements during pregnancy or while breastfeeding.

Contraindications

Do not use if allergic to any ingredients. Inform your doctor if you have a weakened immune system, yeast allergy, liver disease, kidney disease, or peptic ulcers.

Possible side effect

Nutrolin-B Syrup is generally safe but may cause side effects, including digestive discomfort such as bloating, constipation, gas, nausea, and vomiting. These effects are usually mild and resolve over time, but it is important to consult a doctor if they persist.

Drug interaction

Inform your doctor about all medications, especially antifungals, as Nutrolin-B may interact with them.

Missed dose

Take the medicine as soon as you can, but skip the missed dose if it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take two doses at one time.

Overdose

Seek emergency medical attention.
