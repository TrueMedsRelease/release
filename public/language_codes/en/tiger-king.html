Common use

Tiger King medicine contains Sildenafil and used to treat men who have Erectile dysfunction. Tiger King is used to treat men with inability to achieve and maintain penile erection sufficient for satisfactory sexual performance.

Dosage and direction

Take this medicine only as per your physician’s advice. It’s best to take this tablet about 1 hour before the sexual activity with at least one glass of water. Take this tablet on an empty stomach for best results. Do not take this tablet more than 1 in a day.

Precautions

To make sure Tiger King is safe for you, tell your doctor if you have ever had: heart problems (chest pain, a heart rhythm disorder, heart attack); high or low blood pressure; blood circulation problems; retinitis pigmentosa (an inherited condition of the eye); blindness in one or both eyes; bleeding problems; a stomach ulcer; pulmonary veno-occlusive disease (PVOD); liver or kidney disease; a blood cell disorder such as sickle cell anemia, multiple myeloma, or leukemia; a physical deformity of the penis (such as Peyronie's disease); or if you have been told you should not have sexual intercourse for health reasons.

Contraindications

You should not use Tiger King if you are allergic to Sildenafil, or if you take other medicines to treat Erectile dysfunction or Pulmonary arterial hypertension.
Do not take this medicine if you are also using a nitrate drug for chest pain or heart problems. This includes nitroglycerin, isosorbide dinitrate, and isosorbide mononitrate. Nitrates are also found in some recreational drugs such as any nitrite ("poppers").
Avoid alcohol when taking this medicine as it may cause health related problems to you.
This tablet should not be used by men under 18 or over 65 years of age.

Possible side effect

Common Tiger King side effects may include: flushing (warmth, redness, or tingly feeling); headache, dizziness; heartburn, nausea, or upset stomach; abnormal vision (blurred vision, changes in color vision); runny or stuffy nose, nosebleeds; sleep problems (insomnia); or muscle pain, back pain.

Drug interaction

Do not take Tiger King (Sildenafil) with similar medications for Erectile dysfunction, such as avanafil (Stendra), tadalafil (Cialis) or vardenafil (Levitra), or if you take medicines to treat Pulmonary arterial hypertension, such as riociguat (Adempas).
In addition, tell your doctor about all your other medicines, especially: drugs to treat high blood pressure or a prostate disorder; antifungal medicine - ketoconazole or itraconazole; or medicine to treat HIV/AIDS - ritonavir and others.

Missed dose

Since Tiger King is used as needed, you are not likely to miss a dose. But, If you miss a dose, take the medicine as soon as you can, but skip the missed dose if it is almost time for your next dose. Do not take two doses at one time.

Overdose

Seek emergency medical attention.

Storage

Store at room temperature away from moisture and heat. Keep away from children.
