Common use

Dicaris is primarily used as an anti-parasitic medication to treat intestinal worm infections, including roundworms and hookworms. It paralyzes the worms so they can be naturally expelled by the body.

Dosage and direction

Follow your doctor's instructions on the dosage and duration of this medicine. Take it all in at once. It should not be chewed, crushed, or broken. Should take Dicaris with meals.

Precautions

Dicaris should not be used in pregnant women unless the benefits outweigh the risks, as it may cause harm to the foetus.
Dicaris should be used with caution in patients with liver or kidney disease, as dose adjustments may be necessary. Patients with certain blood disorders should also exercise caution while using Dicaris.

Contraindications

Patients with a history of agranulocytosis, leukopenia, or thrombocytopenia should not use Dicaris. Levamisole is also contraindicated in patients with severe liver or kidney disease.
In case of any known allergies to levamisole or any antihelminthics, avoid using this tablet.

Possible side effect

Side effects of Dicaris: Nausea; Headache; Rash; Abdominal pain; Insomnia.

Drug interaction

Dicaris can interact with anticoagulants (such as warfarin, and heparin), and immunosuppressive medications (such as cyclosporine, and tacrolimus).

Missed dose

If you miss a dose of Dicaris, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it is almost time for your next dose, skip the missed one and continue with your regular schedule.

Overdose

Overdose of Dicaris can cause severe neurological effects including confusion, agitation, and seizures. If you suspect an overdose, seek immediate medical help.

Storage

Store Dicaris at room temperature and away from direct sunlight. Do not keep this medication in locations of high moisture, like the bathroom. Keep away from children and pets.
